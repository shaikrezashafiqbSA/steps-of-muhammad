Dhikr Baʿda al-Ṣalāh

Remembrance After the Prayer

📖 The Moment This Was Taught

After every obligatory prayer, the Prophet ﷺ would conclude with the taslim and immediately follow it with this declaration. It was not a private act — ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr (RA) recited it publicly from the Minbar, teaching the ummah exactly as he witnessed it.

The Narrator
Abū al-Zubayr reported:
"I heard ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr speaking from the Minbar, saying: 'When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said the taslim, he would say…'"
The Arabic Narration
سَمِعْتُ I heard
عَبْدَ اللَّهِ ʿAbdullāh
بْنَ الزُّبَيْرِ son of al-Zubayr
يُحَدِّثُ narrating
عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ from the Minbar
كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ the Messenger of Allah ﷺ
إِذَا سَلَّمَ when he said taslim
يَقُولُ would say
Abū al-Zubayr al-Makkī

Full name: Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrus al-Asadī (d. ~126 AH).

A Tābiʿī (Successor generation) scholar from Makkah, known for extensive transmission from the Companions — particularly Jābir ibn ʿAbdullāh (RA).

Regarded as thiqqah (trustworthy) by the majority of hadith scholars. His narrations appear in all six major collections (al-Kutub al-Sittah).

Role here: He transmits from ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr (RA) — a Companion and son of al-Zubayr ibn al-ʿAwwām, one of the ten promised Paradise.

Part 1 · Declaration of Oneness
لَا إِلَهَ there is no god Lā ilāha
إِلَّا اللَّهُ except Allah illā-llāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdahū
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
There is none worthy of worship except Allah alone, with no partner or associate.
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasā'ī, Book 13, Hadith 161 (No. 1339).
Part 2 · His Dominion & Power
لَهُ الْمُلْكُ His is the dominion lahu-l-mulk
وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ to Him all praise wa lahu-l-ḥamd
وَهُوَ عَلَى and He is over wa huwa ʿalā
كُلِّ شَيْءٍ all things kulli shayʾin
قَدِيرٌ all-powerful qadīr
His is the dominion and to Him all praise, and He is able to do all things.
Part 3 · No Power Except with Allah
لَا حَوْلَ there is no power lā ḥawla
وَلَا قُوَّةَ and no strength wa lā quwwata
إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ except with Allah illā bi-llāh
There is no power and no strength except with Allah the Almighty.
Part 4 · Worship of Him Alone
لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ none worthy but Allah Lā ilāha illā-llāh
لَا نَعْبُدُ we worship not lā naʿbudu
إِلَّا إِيَّاهُ except Him alone illā iyyāh
أَهْلَ النِّعْمَةِ source of blessing ahla-n-niʿmah
وَالْفَضْلِ and bounty wa-l-faḍl
وَالثَّنَاءِ الْحَسَنِ and beautiful praise wa-th-thanāʾ il-ḥasan
There is none worthy of worship except Allah, and we worship none but Him — the source of blessing, bounty, and all beautiful praise.
Part 5 · Sincerity Even Against Opposition
لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ none worthy but Allah Lā ilāha illā-llāh
مُخْلِصِينَ لَهُ sincere to Him in mukhlisīna lah
الدِّينَ the religion ad-dīn
وَلَوْ كَرِهَ even if they detest wa law kariha
الْكَافِرُونَ the disbelievers al-kāfirūn
There is none worthy of worship except Allah — and we are sincere in faith and devotion to Him, even though the disbelievers detest it.
✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by Abū al-Zubayr, from ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr (RA). Recorded in Sunan an-Nasā'ī, Book 13, Hadith 161 (No. 1339). Chapter: The tahlīl after the taslim. Grade: Ṣaḥīḥ (Darussalam).
