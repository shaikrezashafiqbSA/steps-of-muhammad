Lā ilāha illa-llāh · waḥdahū · lā sharīka lah

The Prophet’s (ﷺ) remembrance after the taslīm — complete tahlīl, praise, and sincere devotion

📖 Context of this Dhikr

After completing the obligatory prayer and giving the taslīm (salutation of peace), the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) would consistently recite this profound declaration of divine oneness, sovereignty, gratitude, and sincere faith — even when disbelievers detest it. This supplication collects multiple layers of tawḥīd and is a model for post-salah remembrance.

The Narration Chain & Context

Abū al-Zubayr reported:
"I heard ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr speaking from the Minbar, saying: 'When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said the taslīm, he would say (the following duʿā).'"

يُحَدِّثُ narrating yuḥaddithu

وَهُوَ يَقُولُ and he was saying wa-huwa yaqūlu

إِذَا سَلَّمَ when he said the taslīm iḏā sallama
يَقُولُ he would say yaqūlu
"I heard Abdullah ibn az-Zubayr speaking on this minbar, saying: 'When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said the taslīm, he would say: There is none worthy of worship except Allah alone, without any partner. To Him belongs the dominion, to Him is all praise, and He is over all things competent. There is no power nor strength except with Allah the Almighty. There is none worthy of worship except Allah, and we worship none but Him, the source of blessing, favour, and good praise. There is none worthy of worship except Allah, making the religion sincerely for Him even if the disbelievers dislike it.'"
Abū al-Zubayr Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrūs al-Makkī

Full name: Abū al-Zubayr Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrūs al-Qurashī al-Makkī (d. 126 AH / 744 CE). A prominent Successor (Tābiʿī) known for narrating from companions like Jābir ibn ʿAbdillāh, ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar, and ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr.

Hadith critics extensively discussed his tadlīs (concealing weak links), but when he explicitly states "samītu" (I heard) — as here — his narration is considered authentic (sahīh) by leading imams. His transmission is preserved in the Six Books.

Role here: Direct auditory testimony from ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr on the Prophet’s practice. The chain is explicit, avoiding tadlīs, strengthening the reliability of this post-sunnah dhikr.

Part 1 · The Complete Tahlīl & Praise after Taslīm

There is none worthy of worship except Allah alone, without any partner. To Him belongs the dominion, and to Him be all praise, and He is over all things competent. There is no power nor any strength except with Allah the Almighty. There is none worthy of worship except Allah; we worship none but Him, the Source of blessing, favour, and good praise. There is none worthy of worship except Allah, making the religion sincerely for Him, even if the disbelievers detest it.
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasāʾī 1339 · Book 13, Hadith 161 · Vol. 2, Book 13, Hadith 1340
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded by Imām Aḥmad ibn Shuʿayb al-Nasāʾī (215–303 AH) in his Sunan al-Ṣughrā. The chain: Muḥammad ibn Shujāʿ al-Marwadhī ← Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUlayyah ← al-Ḥajjāj ibn Abī ʿUthmān ← Abū al-Zubayr ← ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr ← the Prophet (ﷺ). The narration is declared Ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) by Dārusallām’s editorial verification, and its content is reinforced by parallel narrations in Muslim and Abū Dāwūd. The multiple declarations of tawḥīd, praising Allah’s sole dominion, and announcing sincerity despite opposition form a sunnah dhikr for after the prayer.
