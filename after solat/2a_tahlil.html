The Prophet’s Tahlīl & Praise after Taslīm (End of Prayer)

📖 Context of This Supplication

After completing the obligatory prayer and pronouncing the taslīm (salutation of peace), the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) would habitually recite this powerful declaration of tawḥīd, praise, and sincere devotion. It affirms Allah’s sole dominion, absolute power, and blessing, even in the face of disbelief. This du'ā was taught on the minbar of Madinah and preserved as a daily post-salah practice.

The Narration Chain & Context

Abū al-Zubayr reported:
"I heard ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr speaking from the Minbar, saying: 'When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said the taslīm, he would say (the following du'ā).'"

"Muḥammad ibn Shujāʿ al-Marwadhī informed us, he said: Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUlayyah narrated to us, from al-Ḥajjāj ibn Abī ʿUthmān, he said: Abū al-Zubayr narrated to me, he said: I heard ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr narrating on this minbar, saying: 'When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said the taslīm, he would say...'"
Abū al-Zubayr Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrus al-Makkī

Full name: Abū al-Zubayr Muḥammad ibn Muslim ibn Tadrus al-Asadī al-Makkī (d. ca. 126 AH / 743 CE). A reliable and prolific Meccan narrator, known for his extensive transmissions from the Companion Jābir ibn ʿAbdillāh. He was a client (mawlā) of the Banū Asad and a revered scholar among the tābiʿūn.

Imams Aḥmad and Ibn Saʿd considered him authentic (thiqa), though some critics noted tadlīs (occasional ambiguous transmission). His narrations are foundational in the six major hadith collections. In this chain, he transmits directly from ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr’s public discourse on the minbar, preserving the exact post-salah supplication of the Prophet (ﷺ).

Role here: The pivotal link who heard the ḥadīth from Ibn al-Zubayr and conveyed it to al-Ḥajjāj ibn Abī ʿUthmān, ensuring the chain’s continuity from the Companion generation to later compilers like an-Nasā’ī.

There is none worthy of worship except Allah alone, without any partner. To Him belongs the dominion, and to Him is all praise, and He is over all things all-powerful. There is no power and no strength except with Allah the Almighty.
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasā’ī 1339 – Book 13, Hadith 161 (Vol. 2, Book 13, Hadith 1340)
✅ Source & Authenticity

This ḥadīth is recorded by Imām Aḥmad ibn Shuʿayb an-Nasā’ī in his Sunan (1339). The chain passes through Muḥammad ibn Shujāʿ al-Marwadhī ← Ismāʿīl ibn ʿUlayyah ← al-Ḥajjāj ibn Abī ʿUthmān ← Abū al-Zubayr ← ʿAbdullāh ibn al-Zubayr ← the Prophet (ﷺ). It is graded Ṣaḥīḥ by Dārussalām, based on the reliability of its sub-narrators (excluding any major criticism apart from minor discussion on Abū al-Zubayr’s tadlīs, which is overridden by clear hearing statements). The du'ā is widely practiced after obligatory prayers in many madhāhib.
