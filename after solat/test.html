اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ

The Supplication of Allah's Greatest Name – Sunan Abi Dawud 1493

📖 Occasion & Virtue

Start your dua with this and The Prophet ﷺ heard a man supplicating using this powerful invocation. He then informed the man that he had asked Allah by His Greatest Name — the name by which if asked, He gives, and if called upon, He answers.

The Narration Chain & Context

The Prophet ﷺ heard a man supplicating using this powerful invocation. He then informed the man that he had asked Allah by His Greatest Name — the name by which if asked, He gives, and if called upon, He answers.

Buraydah ibn al-Ḥaṣīb al-Aslamī (رضي الله عنه)

Full name: Buraydah ibn al-Ḥaṣīb ibn ʿAbdillāh ibn al-Ḥārith al-Aslamī. He embraced Islam before the conquest of Khaybar and was a devoted Companion. He died in 63 AH (683 CE) in Marw, Khurasan.

A prominent narrator of ḥadīth, known for his piety and numerous transmissions. He narrated approximately 160 traditions, many of which are recorded in the six major canonical collections. He was a skilled archer and participated in many battles alongside the Prophet ﷺ.

Role here: The original narrator of the incident, transmitting directly from the Prophet ﷺ that the Prophet heard an unnamed man making this supplication and then commented on its immense virtue.

Part 1 · The Supplication of the Greatest Name of Allah
اللَّهُمَّ o allah allāhumma
إِنِّي indeed i innī
أَسْأَلُكَ ask you as'aluka
أَنِّي that i annī
أَشْهَدُ bear witness ashhadu
أَنَّكَ that you annaka
أَنْتَ you anta
اللَّهُ allah llāhu
لَا no lā
إِلَهَ god ilāha
إِلَّا except illā
أَنْتَ you anta
الْأَحَدُ the one al-aḥadu
الصَّمَدُ the eternal refuge al-ṣamadu
الَّذِي who alladhī
لَمْ not lam
يَلِدْ begets yalid
وَلَمْ and not walam
يُولَدْ is begotten yūlad
وَلَمْ and not walam
يَكُنْ is yakun
لَهُ to him lahu
كُفُوًا equal kufuwan
أَحَدٌ anyone aḥad
"O Allah, indeed I ask You, that I bear witness that You are Allah, there is no god except You, the One, the Eternal Refuge, Who has not begotten, nor has been begotten, and there is none equal to Him."
Ṣaḥīḥ (Al-Albānī) Sunan Abī Dāwūd 1493, Book 8, Ḥadīth 78
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded by Abū Dāwūd in his Sunan (no. 1493) from Buraydah ibn al-Ḥaṣīb. Also found in other collections such as Sunan al-Tirmidhī (3544) and Sunan Ibn Mājah (3857) with similar chains. Imam al-Albānī graded this ḥadīth Ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) in Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Abī Dāwūd. It is a powerful supplication invoking Allāh by His Supreme Name (al-Ism al-Aʿẓam), which guarantees acceptance when asked or called upon.
