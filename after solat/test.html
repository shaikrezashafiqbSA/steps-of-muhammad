ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib's Duʿāʾ on ʿArafah

Spiritual Salvation & Worldly Ease · Day of ʿArafah Supplication

📖 Context of This Supplication

The noble companion ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (ra) would recite this comprehensive duʿāʾ on the Day of ʿArafah, seeking both freedom from the Fire and blessings in worldly provision. It beautifully balances spiritual salvation with permissible sustenance while seeking protection from wicked beings among jinn and humankind.

Part 1 · Duʿāʾ of ʿAlī (ra) on ʿArafah
اللَّهُمَّ o allah allāhumma
أَعْتِقْ free aʿtiq
رَقَبَتِي my neck raqabatī
مِنَ from mina
النَّارِ the fire an-nār
وَأَوْسِعْ and expand wa awsiʿ
لِي for me lī
مِنَ from mina
الرِّزْقِ the provision ar-rizqi
الْحَلَالِ the permissible al-ḥalāl
وَاصْرِفْ and avert waṣrif
عَنِّي from me ʿannī
فَسَقَةَ the wicked ones fasaqata
الْجِنِّ of the jinn al-jinn
وَالْإِنسِ and humankind wal-ins
"O Allah, free my neck from the Fire, expand for me from permissible provision, and keep away from me the wicked ones among the jinn and humankind."
Narrated (Non-Prophetic) Al-Suyūṭī, al-Durr al-manthūr, 15:430 · Companion statement

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar's Duʿāʾ & Dhikr on ʿArafah

Takbīr, Praise & Personal Supplication · Day of ʿArafah Practice

📖 Context of This Dhikr & Duʿāʾ

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra), the noble companion and son of the second Caliph, would spend much of the Day of ʿArafah in takbīr and praise of Allah, declaring "Allāhu akbar, Allāhu akbar, wa lillāhi-l-ḥamd." Following this, he would sit in quiet personal supplication—approximately the length of Sūrah al-Fātiḥah—and repeatedly recite this concise yet profound duʿāʾ seeking guidance, piety, and forgiveness in both this life and the next.

Part 1 · The Takbīr & Praise of Allah
اللَّهُ allah allāhu
أَكْبَرُ is the greatest akbaru
اللَّهُ allah allāhu
أَكْبَرُ is the greatest akbaru
وَلِلَّهِ and to allah wa lillāhi
الْحَمْدُ belongs all praise al-ḥamd
"Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest, and to Allah belongs all praise."
Authentic Practice Companion action of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra)
Part 2 · Personal Supplication (Recited Quietly)
اللَّهُمَّ o allah allāhumma
اهْدِنِي guide me ihdinī
بِالْهُدَى with your guidance bil-hudā
وَقِنِي and protect me wa qinī
بِالتَّقْوَى with piety bit-taqwā
وَاغْفِرْ and forgive waghfir
لِي for me lī
فِي in fī
الْآخِرَةِ the next life al-ākhirah
وَالْأُولَى and this life wal-ūlā
"O Allah, guide me with Your guidance, protect me with Your piety, and forgive me in the next life and this life."
Authentic Practice Companion action of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra)
✅ Source & Authenticity Summary

Duʿāʾ of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib (ra): This supplication is recorded by al-Suyūṭī in al-Durr al-manthūr (15:430) as a statement of the noble companion ʿAlī (ra). While not a prophetic hadith, it represents a beautiful and balanced duʿāʾ from one of the most knowledgeable companions, seeking both spiritual salvation and worldly ease. The duʿāʾ is widely cited in Islamic literature on the virtues of ʿArafah [citation:1].

Dhikr & Duʿāʾ of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra): The practice of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra)—spending ʿArafah in takbīr and praise, then sitting in quiet supplication for the length of Sūrah al-Fātiḥah—is documented as an authentic companion practice. His concise duʿāʾ seeking guidance, piety, and forgiveness in both lives reflects the comprehensive nature of supplication on this blessed day.

🔗 Learn More About the Day of ʿArafah

The Day of ʿArafah (9th of Dhul-Ḥijjah) is the holiest day of the Islamic year. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The best of supplication is the supplication of the Day of ʿArafah" (Sunan al-Tirmidhī, no. 3585, graded ḥasan by al-Albānī).
