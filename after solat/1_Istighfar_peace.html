اللهم أنت السلام

Allāhumma Antas-Salām · The Supplication After Obligatory Prayer

📖 Context of Recitation

After completing the obligatory prayer (ṣalāh), before changing position or speaking to others, the Prophet (ﷺ) would seek forgiveness three times and then recite this profound du'ā, affirming Allāh as the source of perfect peace and blessing.

The Narration Chain & Context

Thawbān reported:
"When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) finished his prayer, he begged forgiveness three times and said: O Allah! Thou art Peace, and peace comes from Thee; Blessed art Thou, O Possessor of Glory and Honour."

Dāwud ibn Rushayd narrated to us, al-Walīd narrated to us from al-Awzāʿī from Abū ʿAmmār, whose name is Shaddād ibn ʿAbdillāh, from Abū Asmāʾ from Thawbān, who said: When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) finished his prayer, he sought forgiveness three times and said: "O Allah, You are Peace, and from You is peace, blessed are You, O Possessor of Majesty and Honour." Al-Walīd said: I asked al-Awzāʿī: "How is seeking forgiveness?" He replied: "You say: Astaghfirullāh, Astaghfirullāh."
Thawbān (ثوبان) – The Freed Slave of the Messenger of Allah

Full name: Thawbān ibn Bujdud, Abū ‘Abdillāh (or Abū ‘Abd al-Raḥmān). Originally from Syria or Yemen, he was taken as a slave and then freed by the Prophet (ﷺ). He died in the year 54 AH (674 CE) in Hims (Homs), Syria.

Thawbān served the Prophet (ﷺ) closely for several years and was known for his deep devotion and knowledge of fiqh. He narrated approximately 40 hadith, which appear in all six major canonical collections (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Muslim, Abū Dāwūd, al-Tirmidhī, al-Nasā’ī, Ibn Mājah). He was a reliable transmitter (thiqqah) with a direct, personal connection to the Prophet.

Role here: Thawbān is the primary narrator of this hadith, reporting directly from the Prophet (ﷺ) about his post-prayer practice. His testimony establishes the sunnah of saying "Astaghfirullāh" three times followed by the "Allāhumma Antas-Salām" supplication.

Part 1 · The Threefold Seeking of Forgiveness (Istighfār)
أَسْتَغْفِرُ i seek forgiveness astaghfiru
اللَّهَ allāh allāha
I seek forgiveness from Allah. (repeated three times)
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Book 5, Hadīth 171 (Sunnah.com ref: Book 4, Hadīth 1226)

✅ Source & Authenticity

This ḥadīth is recorded by Imām Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj in his Ṣaḥīḥ (Book 5, The Book of Mosques and Places of Prayer, Chapter 26: The Recommendation of Remembrances After Prayer). The chain is authentic (ṣaḥīḥ) through al-Awzāʿī, Abū ʿAmmār (Shaddād ibn ʿAbdillāh), Abū Asmāʾ, and Thawbān. Muslim scholars unanimously consider this narration sound and its practice a confirmed sunnah.
