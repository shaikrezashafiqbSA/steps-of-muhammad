Lā ilāha illā-llāh waḥdahū

The Prophet’s Tahlīl & Praise after Taslīm (End of Prayer)

📖 Context of Recitation

Upon completing the obligatory prayer and pronouncing the taslīm, the Prophet ﷺ would routinely recite this comprehensive declaration of Allah’s oneness, sovereignty, and absolute power. It is a sunnah act of dhikr that affirms tawḥīd and seeks refuge from any deprivation of divine generosity.

The Narration Chain & Context

Mughīrah ibn Shuʿbah wrote to Muʿāwiyah:
"When the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) finished the prayer and pronounced the salutation, he would utter (this supplication)..."

Al-Mughīrah ibn Shuʿbah (الْمُغِيرَةُ بْنُ شُعْبَةَ)

Full name: Abū ʿAbd Allāh al-Mughīrah ibn Shuʿbah ibn Abī ʿĀmir ibn Masʿūd ath-Thaqafī. Companion of the Prophet ﷺ, governor of Basra and Kufa, died 50 AH / 670 CE.

A highly intelligent and influential Thaqafī leader who embraced Islam after the Battle of the Trench. Known for his deep loyalty to the early caliphs and for transmitting several key aḥādīth on prayer, purification, and statecraft.

Role here: The original narrator who wrote to Muʿāwiyah preserving the exact post-prayer duʿā of the Prophet ﷺ, forming the basis of this ṣaḥīḥ report.

✅ Source & Authenticity

This duʿā is transmitted by Mughīrah ibn Shuʿbah via a chain ending with Warrād. Recorded by Imām Muslim in his Ṣaḥīḥ (Kitāb al-Masājid wa Mawāḍiʿ al-Ṣalāh, Bāb istiḥbāb al-dhikr baʿd al-ṣalāh wa bayān sifatih). The chain is Ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) according to Muslim’s rigorous criteria and unanimously accepted by the major hadith scholars. The Prophet ﷺ would recite this immediately after the taslīm of every obligatory prayer.
