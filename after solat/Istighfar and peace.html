اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ السَّلَامُ وَمِنْكَ السَّلَامُ

Allāhumma Anta as-Salām wa Minka as-Salām

📖 After the Ṣalām – The Prophet’s ﷺ Immediate Practice

Thawbān (RA) reports that after completing every prayer, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ would seek forgiveness three times, then recite this powerful declaration. This du'ā affirms that all true peace and safety belong to Allah alone, and He is the source of all peace. It is a sunnah that transforms the moments after ṣalāh into a profound act of worship.

The Narration Chain

Thawbān (RA) reported:
"When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ finished his prayer, he would seek forgiveness three times and then say: O Allah, You are Peace and from You comes peace. Blessed are You, O Owner of majesty and honor."
The Arabic Narration (partial chain)
حَدَّثَنَا narrated to us ḥaddathanā
دَاوُدُ بْنُ رُشَيْدٍ Dāwūd ibn Rushayd Dāwūdu bnu Rushayd
عَنِ الْوَلِيدِ from al-Walīd ʿani-l-Walīd
عَنِ الأَوْزَاعِيِّ from al-Awzāʿī ʿani-l-Awzāʿī
عَنْ أَبِي عَمَّارٍ from Abū ʿAmmār ʿan Abī ʿAmmār
The chain: Dāwūd ibn Rushayd → al-Walīd → al-Awzāʿī → Abū ʿAmmār (Shaddād ibn ʿAbdullāh) → Abū Asmā’ → Thawbān (RA).
Thawbān (RA) – مَوْلَى رَسُولِ اللَّهِ

Full name: Thawbān ibn Bujdud (d. ~54 AH), a freed slave of the Prophet ﷺ.

A noble Companion who served the Prophet ﷺ closely. Renowned for his deep knowledge and piety. Lived in Hims (Syria) and transmitted many key aḥādīth. Scholars unanimously regard him as trustworthy (thiqqah).

Role here: Direct eyewitness reporting the Prophet’s ﷺ post-prayer practice — a primary source for this beloved du'ā.

Part 1 · Seeking Forgiveness (Al-Istighfār) x 3
أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ I seek forgiveness from Allah Astaghfiru-llāh
“I seek Allah’s forgiveness x 3”
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Book 5 (Masājid), Hadith 171 (No. 591).
Part 2 · Declaration of All Peace
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
أَنْتَ You are Anta
السَّلَامُ the Peace as-Salām
وَمِنْكَ and from You wa Minka
السَّلَامُ is peace as-Salām
“O Allah, You are Peace, and from You comes peace.”
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 591

ذَا O Possessor of Dhā
الْجَلَالِ majesty al-Jalāl
وَ and wa

“Blessed are You, O Owner of majesty and honour.”
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 591
✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by Thawbān (RA), the freed servant of the Prophet ﷺ. Recorded by Imām Muslim in his Ṣaḥīḥ, Book of Mosques and Places of Prayer (Kitāb al-Masājid), Hadith 591. Graded as Ṣaḥīḥ by unanimous consensus of hadith scholars. Also reported by al-Tirmidhī, Abū Dāwūd, and Aḥmad with similar wording.
