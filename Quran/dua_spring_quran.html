اللهم إني عبدك • Allāhumma innī ‘abduk

Du‘ā for the Relief of Anxiety, Sorrow, and Distress – Fortress of the Muslim (Hisn al-Muslim 120)

📖 When & Why to Recite

This profound supplication was taught by the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ to be recited during moments of overwhelming worry, sorrow, or hardship. It affirms complete servitude to Allah, acknowledges His absolute decree, and invokes all of His magnificent names — including those known only to Him — to ask that the Qur’ān becomes the spring of the heart, the light of the chest, and the banisher of grief. A comprehensive remedy for spiritual and emotional distress.

Part 1 · The Complete Supplication of Servitude & Relief

أَوِ or awi
اسْتَـأْثَرْتَ you kept exclusively ista’tharta
بِهِ with it bihi
فِي in fī
عِلْمِ the knowledge ‘ilmi
الغَيْـبِ of the unseen al-ghaybi

أَنْ that an
تَجْـعَلَ you make taj‘ala
القُرْآنَ the qur’ān al-qur’āna
رَبِيـعَ spring rabī‘a

وَنورَ and light wa nūra

وجَلَاءَ and banisher wa jalā’a
حُـزْنِي of my sadness ḥuznī
وذَهَابَ and reliever wa dhahāba
هَمِّـي of my distress hammī
O Allah, I am Your slave, and the son of Your male slave, and the son of your female slave. My forehead is in Your Hand (You have full control over me). Your Judgment upon me is assured, and Your Decree concerning me is just. I ask You by every Name that You have named Yourself with, revealed in Your Book, taught any one of Your creation, or kept unto Yourself in the knowledge of the unseen that is with You, to make the Qur’ān the spring of my heart, and the light of my chest, the banisher of my sadness, and the reliever of my distress.
Ṣaḥīḥ Musnad Aḥmad (1/391) • Authenticated by Shaykh al-Albānī as ṣaḥīḥ (Hisn al-Muslim 120)
✅ Source & Authenticity

This du‘ā is recorded in Musnad Aḥmad (1/391) and included in Fortress of the Muslim (Hisn al-Muslim, ḥadīth 120). Imām al-Albānī verified its chain as ṣaḥīḥ (authentic). The supplication embodies tawḥīd, total reliance on Allāh, and the therapeutic power of the Qur’ān over emotional burdens.
