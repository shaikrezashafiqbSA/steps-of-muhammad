# Prophetic Supplications for Pain and Healing
### Authentic Remedies from the Sunnah
## 📖 Islamic Medicine Context
Seeking spiritual remedies (*Ruqyah*) alongside physical medical treatment represents the comprehensive approach of the Sunnah. These prayers demonstrate how the Prophet ﷺ and his companions actively sought relief from severe physical afflictions by placing their hands on the location of pain and appealing directly to Allah's absolute power and mercy.
## 1. The Remedy for Localised Body Pain
### Method of Practice
 1. Place your right hand directly on the area of the face or body where the pain is located.
 2. Say **"Bismillah"** (In the name of Allah) **three times**.
 3. Recite the supplication below **seven times**.
### The Supplication
> ### أَعُوذُ بِعِزَّةِ اللَّهِ وَقُدْرَتِهِ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا أَجِدُ وَأُحَاذِرُ
> **Transliteration:** *Aʿūdhu bi-ʿizzatillāhi wa-qudratihī min sharri mā ajidu wa-uḥādhir.*
> **Translation:** "I seek refuge in the might and power of Allah from the evil of what I feel and fear."
> 
### Word-by-Word Breakdown
| Arabic | Transliteration | English Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **أَعُوذُ** | Aʿūdhu | I seek refuge |
| **بِعِزَّةِ اللَّهِ** | bi-ʿizzatillāh | in Allah's might |
| **وَقُدْرَتِهِ** | wa-qudratihī | and His power |
| **مِنْ شَرِّ** | min sharri | from the evil |
| **مَا أَجِدُ** | mā ajidu | of what I find and feel |
| **وَأُحَاذِرُ** | wa-uḥādhir | and I fear / guard against |
### 🔍 Source & Prescribed Story
 * **Hadith Reference:** Sahih Muslim, Book 39, Hadith 2202.
 * **Authenticity Grade:** **Ṣaḥīḥ** (Authentic)
 * **The Story Behind It:** This du'a was taught by the Prophet ﷺ to a companion named **Uthman ibn Abi al-As al-Thaqafi**. Uthman came to the Prophet ﷺ complaining of an agonizing, chronic pain in his body that had severely afflicted him ever since he embraced Islam. The Prophet ﷺ instructed him to place his hand directly over the painful site and repeat this specific formula. Uthman later reported: *"I did that, and Allah removed what I was experiencing, and I never ceased to command my family and others to do the same."*
## 2. The Comprehensive Prayer of Shifa (Healing)
### Method of Practice
Recite this prayer while gently sweeping or holding your right hand over the person who is sick, or over your own face and jawline.
### The Supplication
> ### اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ النَّاسِ أَذْهِبِ الْبَأْسَ اشْفِ أَنْتَ الشَّافِي لَا شِفَاءَ إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ شِفَاءً لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا
> **Transliteration:** *Allāhumma Rabba-nṇās, adhhibi-l-ba's, Ishfi Anta ash-Shāfī, lā shifā'a illā shifā'uka, shifā'an lā yughādiru saqamā.*
> **Translation:** "O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the harm. Heal, for You are the Healer. There is no healing except Your healing—a healing that leaves no disease behind."
> 
### Word-by-Word Breakdown
| Arabic | Transliteration | English Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **اللَّهُمَّ** | Allāhumma | O Allah |
| **رَبَّ النَّاسِ** | Rabba-nṇās | Lord of mankind |
| **أَذْهِبِ الْبَأْسَ** | adhhibi-l-ba's | remove the harm / affliction |
| **اشْفِ** | Ishfi | heal / cure |
| **أَنْتَ الشَّافِي** | Anta ash-Shāfī | You are the Healer |
| **لَا شِفَاءَ** | lā shifā'a | there is no cure / healing |
| **إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ** | illā shifā'uka | except Your cure |
| **شِفَاءً** | shifā'an | a healing |
| **لَا يُغَادِرُ سَقَمًا** | lā yughādiru saqamā | that leaves behind no illness |
### 🔍 Source & Prescribed Story
 * **Hadith Reference:** Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 76, Hadith 5743 & Sahih Muslim, Book 39, Hadith 2191.
 * **Authenticity Grade:** **Ṣaḥīḥ** (Authentic)
 * **The Story Behind It:** Narrated by **Aisha (RA)**, the wife of the Prophet ﷺ. She noted that this was the definitive household practice of the Prophet ﷺ. Whenever a member of his family fell ill with a fever, severe pain, or disease, the Prophet ﷺ would immediately go to them, rub them with his right hand to offer physical comfort, and recite this beautiful prayer. It demonstrates his profound empathy, showing that he combined physical touch, warmth, and an unshakeable reliance on divine intervention when dealing with severe physical suffering.
