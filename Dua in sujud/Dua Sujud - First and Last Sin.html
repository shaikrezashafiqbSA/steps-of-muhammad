Du'ā — Allāhumma-ghfir lī dhanbī

Supplication in Prostration (Sujūd)

This comprehensive supplication for forgiveness was frequently recited by the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) during prostration (sujūd). Prostration is the state where a slave is closest to their Lord, making it an ideal time for earnest seeking of forgiveness. This du'ā is unique in its "all-encompassing" structure, pairing opposites to ensure no sin is left out.

Part 1 · The General Plea
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
اغْفِرْ forgive
لِي for me
ذَنْبِي my sin
كُلَّهُ all of it
Allāhumma-ghfir lī dhanbī kullah
O Allah, forgive me all my sins
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported in Sahih Muslim. The word 'kullah' (all of it) serves as a general opening before the specific breakdown follows.
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 483 ↗
Part 2 · Magnitude & Time
دِقَّهُ the small
وَجِلَّهُ and the great
diqqahu wa jillahu
The small and the great (sins)
Ṣaḥīḥ Diqq refers to subtle, minute sins, while Jill refers to major or obvious sins.
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 483 ↗
وَأَوَّلَهُ the first
وَآخِرَهُ and the last
wa awwalahu wa ākhirahu
The first and the last (of them)
Ṣaḥīḥ Seeking forgiveness for sins committed in the distant past and those committed recently or yet to come.
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 483 ↗
Part 3 · Manifestation
وَعَلاَنِيَتَهُ the open
وَسِرَّهُ and the secret
wa ʿalāniyatahu wa sirrahu
The open and the secret (sins)
Ṣaḥīḥ Concluding with sins done in public view and those hidden within the heart or done in private.
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 483 ↗

This hadith is recorded in the Book of Prayer within Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, one of the two most authentic collections of prophetic traditions.

Ṣaḥīḥ Full Reference: Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 483 ↗

Abu Huraira reported that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) used to say this while prostrating himself.
