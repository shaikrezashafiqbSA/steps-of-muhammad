Du'ā — Opening of Prayer

Al-Istiftāḥ: Seeking Purification

This is the Du'ā al-Istiftāḥ (Opening Supplication) which the Prophet (ﷺ) would recite silently after the opening Takbīr and before starting the recitation of Sūrat al-Fātiḥah. It uses powerful metaphors of distance and physical washing to plea for the total removal of the spiritual burden of sins.

Part 1 · Distance from Sins
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
بَاعِدْ distance
بَيْنِي between me
وَبَيْنَ and between
خَطَايَاىَ my sins
كَمَا as
بَاعَدْتَ You distanced
بَيْنَ between
الْمَشْرِقِ the East
وَالْمَغْرِبِ and the West
Allāhumma bāʿid baynī wa bayna khaṭāyāya kamā bāʿadta baynal-mashriqi wal-maghrib
O Allah! Set me apart from my sins as the East and West are set apart from each other.
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported by Abu Huraira in the context of the prayer's opening silence.
Sahih al-Bukhari 744 ↗
Part 2 · Cleansing
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
نَقِّنِي cleanse me
مِنَ from
الْخَطَايَا the sins
كَمَا as
يُنَقَّى is cleaned
الثَّوْبُ the garment
الأَبْيَضُ white
مِنَ from
الدَّنَسِ the dirt
Allāhumma naqqinī minal-khaṭāyā kamā yunaqqath-thawbul-abyaḍu minad-danas
O Allah! Clean me from sins as a white garment is cleaned of dirt.
Ṣaḥīḥ The white garment metaphor emphasizes the purity and clarity of the heart after forgiveness.
Sahih al-Bukhari 744 ↗
Part 3 · Washing
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
اغْسِلْ wash
خَطَايَاىَ my sins
بِالْمَاءِ with water
وَالثَّلْجِ and snow
وَالْبَرَدِ and hail
Allāhumma-ghsil khaṭāyāya bil-mā'i wath-thalji wal-barad
O Allah! Wash off my sins with water, snow, and hail.
Ṣaḥīḥ Washing with cold elements signifies extinguishing the "heat" and traces of transgression.
Sahih al-Bukhari 744 ↗

This supplication is one of several narrated for the opening of prayer. It is recorded in the Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim, making it of the highest level of authenticity.

Ṣaḥīḥ Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari 744 ↗

Abu Huraira asked the Prophet (ﷺ) about his silence before the recitation, to which he replied with this du'ā.
