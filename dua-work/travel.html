Duʿāʾ ar-Rukūb wa s-Safar

Supplications of the Prophet ﷺ when mounting a transport and during travel

📖 Context of the Hadith

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra) reports that whenever the Messenger of Allah ﷺ intended to travel and mounted his riding camel, he would glorify Allah, recite specific verses of the Qur’an, and supplicate for righteousness, safety, and family protection. Upon returning, he would utter another beautiful duʿā of gratitude and repentance.

📜 The Narration Chain & Full Context

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar (ra) narrated:
"When the Prophet (ﷺ) wanted to travel, when he mounted his riding camel, he would say the Takbir three times and say: 'Glory is to Him Who has subjected this to us, and we were not able to do it. And, surely, to our Lord are we returning.' Then he would say: 'O Allah, I ask You in this journey of mine from the righteousness and piety and actions that which You are pleased with. O Allah, ease for us the path, and make near for us the distance of the land. O Allah, You are the companion in the journey, and the caretaker for the family. O Allah, accompany us in our journey, and take care of our families.' And when he would return to his family, he would say: '(We are) Returning, if Allah wills, repenting, worshipping, and to our Lord directing the praise.'"

"From Ibn ʿUmar that the Prophet (ﷺ), when he traveled and mounted his riding camel, he said Allāhu Akbar three times and said: 'Glory be to Him Who has subjected this to us, and we were not capable of it, and indeed to our Lord we are returning.' Then he said: 'O Allah, I ask You in this journey of mine from righteousness, piety, and from actions that which You are pleased with. O Allah, make the journey easy for us and shorten the distance of the land. O Allah, You are the Companion in travel and the Caretaker for the family. O Allah, accompany us in our journey and take care of our families.' And when he returned to his family he would say: 'Returning, if Allah wills, repenting, worshipping, and to our Lord directing praise.'"
ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (ra)

Full name: ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb al-ʿAdawī (d. 74 AH / 693 CE). Companion of the Prophet ﷺ, renowned for his strict adherence to the Sunnah, vast knowledge of fiqh and hadith. He narrated over 2,600 traditions.

Role here: Direct narrator from the Prophet ﷺ, transmitting the authentic practice of supplication during travel. His reports are foundational in the chapters of adhkār and duʿā.

Authenticity: This hadith is graded Ṣaḥīḥ (Darussalam) and recorded by Imām at-Tirmidhī.
