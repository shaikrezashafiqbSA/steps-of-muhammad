Du'ā al-Istis'āb

For One Whose Affairs Have Become Difficult

📖 Context of Recitation

This supplication is recited when a person encounters hardships, complexities, or challenging situations in life. It serves as an ultimate reliance on Allah, acknowledging that ease and the resolution of hardships come solely through His divine will.

Part 1 · Core Supplication
اللَّهُمَّ o Allah Allāhumma
لَا سَهْلَ there is no ease lā saḥla
إِلَّا مَا except what illā mā
جَعَلْتَهُ you have made it jaʿaltahu
سَهْلًا easy sahlan
وَأَنْتَ and you wa anta
تَجْعَلُ make tajʿalu
الْحَزْنَ the sorrow al-ḥazna
إِذَا شِئْتَ if you please idhā shi'ta
سَهْلًا easy sahlan
O Allah, there is no ease other than what You make easy, and If You please You ease sorrow.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ibn Hibban, Sahih, Hadith 2427 (No. 2427).
✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by Anas ibn Malik (RA). Recorded by Ibn Hibban in his Sahih (no. 2427) and Ibn As-Sunni (no. 351). Grade: Ṣaḥīḥ (Authentic) according to Al-Hafidh Ibn Hajar and 'Abdul-Qadir Al-Arna'ut.
