Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min al-hammi wal-ḥazani

O Allah, I seek refuge with You from worry, grief, laziness, miserliness, cowardice, difficult debt and being overpowered by men

📖 Context of the Supplication

This comprehensive duʿā was regularly recited by the Prophet Muḥammad (ﷺ) to seek Allah’s protection from seven debilitating spiritual and worldly afflictions. It combines inner emotional distress (worry, grief, laziness) with destructive character traits (miserliness, cowardice) and external hardships (overwhelming debt, oppression by others). The Prophet (ﷺ) taught this as a daily morning and evening supplication to guard a believer’s heart and circumstances.

The Narration Chain & Context

Anas ibn Mālik reported:
"The Prophet [SAW] used to say: 'O Allah, I seek refuge with You from worry, grief, laziness, miserliness, cowardice, difficult debt and being overpowered by men.'"

Anas ibn Mālik (أَنَس بْن مَالِك)

Full name: Anas ibn Mālik ibn an-Naḍr al-Khazrajī an-Najjārī (c. 612 – 712 CE / 8 BH – 93 AH). He served as a servant to Prophet Muḥammad (ﷺ) for ten years from age 10, becoming one of the most prolific narrators of ḥadīth with over 2,000 narrations.

Known for his meticulous memory and eyewitness accounts of the Prophet’s daily life, prayers, and supplications. He was the last of the major Companions to die in Basra. His narrations are found in all six canonical ḥadīth collections, with al-Bukhārī and Muslim recording over 300 of his reports.

Role here: Direct Companion transmitter who heard this duʿā repeatedly from the Prophet (ﷺ) in various settings, then passed it to ʿAmr ibn Abī ʿAmr, forming the chain used by an-Nasāʾī.

✅ Source & Authenticity

This ḥadīth is recorded by Imām Aḥmad ibn Shuʿayb an-Nasāʾī (214–303 AH) in his Sunan, one of the six canonical ḥadīth collections. The chain is ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) as graded by Dārus-Salām and multiple ḥadīth scholars. The narration comes through the reliable path: Aḥmad ibn Ḥarb → Al-Qāsim ibn Yazīd al-Jarmī → ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz → ʿAmr ibn Abī ʿAmr → Anas ibn Mālik. Anas (ra) explicitly heard this duʿā from the Prophet (ﷺ) and reported its regular usage. The seven items covered are both internal spiritual ailments and external worldly trials, making this a comprehensive morning/evening supplication.
