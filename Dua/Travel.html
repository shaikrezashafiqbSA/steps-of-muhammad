Du'ā al-Safar

The Prayer for Travel & Journeys

Safar (سفر) refers to travelling or embarking on a journey. This sunnah supplication is recited when mounting a vehicle or beginning a trip by any means—be it car, plane, or ship—to seek Allah's protection and barakah.

Part 1 · The Opening
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ In the name of Allah
(Bismillah) ×3
In the name of Allah
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ Praise be to Allah
Alhamdulillah
All praise is due to Allah
Part 2 · The Core Supplication
سُبْحَانَ Glory be to
الَّذِي the One who
سَخَّرَ has subjected
لَنَا to us
هَٰذَا this
وَمَا and not
كُنَّا were we
لَهُ to it
مُقْرِنِينَ capable (of controlling)
Subhān-alladhi sakh-khara lana hadha wa ma kunna lahu muqrinīn
Glory be to the One who has placed this (transport) at our service, and we ourselves would not have been capable of that.
Qur'ān From Sūrat az-Zukhruf, Āyāt 13-14.
وَإِنَّا And indeed we
إِلَىٰ to
رَبِّنَا our Lord
لَمُنقَلِبُونَ are surely returning
Wa inna ila Rabbina lamunqalibūn
And surely, to our Lord we are returning.
Part 3 · Praises & Seeking Forgiveness
Alhamdulillah (×3) & Allāhu Akbar (×3)
All praise is due to Allah & Allah is the Greatest
سُبْحَانَكَ Glory be to You
إِنِّي indeed I
ظَلَمْتُ have wronged
نَفْسِي myself
فَاغْفِرْ لِي so forgive me
فَإِنَّهُ for indeed
لَا يَغْفِرُ none forgives
الذُّنُوبَ sins
إِلَّا أَنْتَ except You
Subhanaka inni qad zalamtu nafsi faghfirli fa-innahu la yaghfirudh-dhunuba illa Ant
Glory is to You, indeed I have wronged myself, so forgive me, for indeed none forgives sins except You.
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported in Jami' At-Tirmidhi, Hadith 3446.

This full sequence is recorded in Jami' At-Tirmidhi, Vol. 6, Book of Supplications, Hadith 3446. It was narrated that Ali (RA) recited this upon mounting his animal, laughing afterward because the Prophet ﷺ had done the same to show Allah's pleasure with a servant seeking forgiveness.
