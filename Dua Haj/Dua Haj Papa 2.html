Practices of the First Ten Days of Dhul Ḥijjah

File 1 of 3 · Tahlīl & Shahāda Variants · Sections 1–4

📖 The Blessed Ten Days

The first ten days of Dhul Ḥijjah are among the most virtuous days of the year. The Prophet ﷺ said that no good deeds done on other days are superior to those done during these ten days. This collection gathers the adhkār, du'ās, and ṣalawāt recommended for this blessed period.

Section 1 · Tahlīl with Full Divine Attributes
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdah
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
لَهُ الْمُلْكُ His is the dominion lahul-mulk
وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ and His is the praise wa lahul-ḥamd
يُحْيِي He gives life yuḥyī
وَيُمِيتُ and causes death wa yumīt
وَهُوَ حَيٌّ and He is Ever-Living wa huwa ḥayyun
لَا يَمُوتُ He does not die lā yamūt
بِيَدِهِ الْخَيْرُ in His hand is all good biyadihil-khayr
وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ and He is over all things wa huwa 'alā kulli shay'in
قَدِيرٌ all-powerful Qadīr
There is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner. His is all dominion and all praise. He gives life and causes death — Ever-Living, undying, in Whose hand is all good, over all things Able.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 80, Hadith 6403. Also in Jāmiʿ at-Tirmidhī, Book 45, Hadith 3474.
Section 2 · Shahāda with Qur'ānic Attributes
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ I bear witness: no god but Allah Ashhadu an lā ilāha illallāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdah
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
إِلَٰهًا وَاحِدًا one God ilāhan wāḥidā
أَحَدًا singular aḥadā
صَمَدًا the Eternal Refuge ṣamadā
لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ He has not taken lam yattakhidh
صَاحِبَةً a spouse ṣāḥibah
وَلَا وَلَدًا nor a child wa lā waladā
I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner — one God, singular, the Eternal Refuge, Who has taken neither spouse nor child.
Qur'ān Combines the Shahāda with attributes drawn from Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ (112:2) and Sūrat al-Jinn (72:3). Formulation found in Bā'alawī awrād compilations.
Section 3 · Shahāda with Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ I bear witness: no god but Allah Ashhadu an lā ilāha illallāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdah
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
أَحَدٌ صَمَدٌ the One, the Eternal Aḥadun Ṣamad
لَمْ يَلِدْ He did not beget lam yalid
وَلَمْ يُولَدْ nor was He begotten wa lam yūlad
وَلَمْ يَكُنْ لَهُ and there is not for Him wa lam yakun lah
كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ any equal whatsoever kufuwan Aḥad
I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner — the One, the Eternal Refuge. He did not beget, nor was He begotten, and there is none equal to Him.
Qur'ān Shahāda prefixed to the full text of Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ (112), Āyāt 1–4.
