Takbir al-ʿĪd (ʿĪd al-Aḍḥā)

The Magnification of Allāh · Word‑for‑Word Recitation

📖 When to Recite this Takbir

The restricted (muqayyad) takbir for ʿĪd al‑Aḍḥā begins after the Fajr prayer on the day of ʿArafah (9th Dhū al‑Ḥijjah) and continues until the ʿAṣr prayer of the 13th of Dhū al‑Ḥijjah, after every obligatory prayer. It is also Sunnah to proclaim it loudly while travelling to the ʿĪd prayer. This practice was the consensus of the Companions, including ʿUmar, ʿAlī, Ibn Masʿūd and Ibn ʿAbbās (may Allāh be pleased with them all).

The Transmission Chain & Scholarly Source

ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd reported:
“Ibn Masʿūd, may Allāh be pleased with him, used to recite the takbīr during the days of tashrīq saying… ”

“On the authority of ʿAbdillāh ibn Masʿūd (may Allah be pleased with him): that he used to say the takbīr during the days of Tashrīq: ‘Allāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar, Lā ilāha illā-llāh, wa-llāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar, wa lillāhi-l-ḥamd.’”
ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd (d. 32 AH / 652 CE)

Full name: ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd ibn Ghāfil ibn Ḥabīb al-Hudhalī. A Companion of the Prophet ﷺ, renowned for his profound knowledge of the Qur’ān and Ḥadīth. He was the sixth person to embrace Islam publicly and a close attendant of the Prophet, often mistaken for a member of the Prophet’s household due to his intimate companionship.

He served as a governor of Kufa under ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb. His transmission of Islamic sciences is among the most authoritative, and his recitation of the Qur’ān was praised directly by the Prophet ﷺ. He died in Medina in 32 AH.

Role here: The takbīr wording is authentically transmitted from him through multiple chains in the muṣannaf works of Ibn Abī Shaybah, al-Bayhaqī, and al-Ḥākim. His practice was emulated by the early Muslim community during the days of tashrīq.

✅ Source & Authenticity

This word‑for‑word recitation is the Takbīr al-Tashrīq transmitted from ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd (may Allāh be pleased with him) with a ṣaḥīḥ chain. Recorded by Ibn Abī Shaybah (Muṣannaf, 2/165–168), al-Bayhaqī (Sunan al-Kubrā), and authenticated by al-Albānī in Irwāʾ al‑Ghalīl (3/125, no. 654). It is recited after each obligatory prayer from Fajr of ʿArafah (9th Dhū al‑Ḥijjah) until ʿAṣr of the 13th of Dhū al‑Ḥijjah, and loudly on the way to the ʿĪd prayer.
