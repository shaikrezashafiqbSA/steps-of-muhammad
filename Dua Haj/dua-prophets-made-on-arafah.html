Lā ilāha illallāh waḥdahū lā sharīka lah

The Supreme Invocation of the Day of 'Arafah · Best Words of the Prophets

📖 The Day of Hope & Forgiveness

The Day of 'Arafah (9th of Dhul-Hijjah) is the holiest day of the Islamic year. On this day, pilgrims stand in prayer on the plain of 'Arafah, and non-pilgrims are encouraged to fast and increase in supplication. The Prophet ﷺ declared that the best du'ā is the du'ā of 'Arafah, and the most excellent words ever spoken by any prophet are this declaration of Allah's absolute oneness, dominion, and power.

The Narration Chain & Context

'Abdullāh ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ reported that the Prophet ﷺ said:
"The best invocation is that of the Day of 'Arafah, and the best that anyone can say is what I and the Prophets before me have said: Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahū lā sharīka lah, lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamdu wa huwa `alā kulli shay'in qadīr."

"'Abdullāh ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: 'The best supplication is the supplication of the Day of 'Arafah, and the best that I and the Prophets before me have said is: There is none worthy of worship but Allah alone, without partner, to Him belongs the dominion and to Him belongs all praise, and He is over all things competent.'"
'Abdullāh ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ (عَبْدُ اللَّهِ بْنُ عَمْرِو بْنِ الْعَاصِ)

Full name: 'Abdullāh ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ ibn Wā’il as-Sahmī al-Qurashī (d. 65 AH / 684 CE). A distinguished companion of the Prophet ﷺ, renowned for his vast knowledge and devotion to writing the prophetic traditions. He was a prolific narrator who lived long after the Prophet, preserving many aḥādīth concerning du'ā and worship.

His collection of narrations is widely cited in the major ḥadīth compilations. Imams Aḥmad, al-Bukhārī, Muslim, and the authors of the Sunan all transmitted from him. He was known as 'the scribe of the Prophet' and was one of the few companions who recorded ḥadīth during the Prophet's lifetime.

Role here: He transmits this foundational narration regarding the superiority of du'ā on the Day of 'Arafah directly from the Prophet ﷺ, forming the primary chain for this teaching in the works of At-Tirmidhī and others.

Ḥasan · Ṣaḥīḥ At-Tirmidhī (3585) · Al-Albānī: Ṣaḥīḥ at-Tirmidhī 3/184 & Silsilat al-Āḥādīth aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥah 4/6 (no. 1503)
✅ Source & Authenticity

This magnificent du'ā is recorded by Imām At-Tirmidhī in his Jāmi' (no. 3585) on the authority of 'Abdullāh ibn 'Amr ibn al-'Āṣ. Shaykh Muḥammad Nāṣir ad-Dīn al-Albānī graded it as Ḥasan (good) in Ṣaḥīḥ at-Tirmidhī (3/184) and further authenticated it in Silsilat al-Āḥādīth aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥah (vol. 4, p. 6, no. 1503). The text is also cited in Hisn al-Muslim (Fortress of the Muslim, no. 237) as the essential supplication for the Day of 'Arafah.

The phrase “the best that I and the Prophets before me have said” elevates this declaration above all others — it is the core of tawḥīd (monotheism) shared by every messenger from Nūḥ to Muḥammad ﷺ. Muslims are encouraged to recite it abundantly on the 9th of Dhul-Hijjah, especially during the afternoon hours.
