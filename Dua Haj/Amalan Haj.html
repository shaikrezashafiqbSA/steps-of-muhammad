📖 The Blessed Ten Days

The first ten days of Dhul Ḥijjah are among the most virtuous days of the year. The Prophet ﷺ said that no good deeds done on other days are superior to those done during these ten days. This collection gathers the adhkār, du'ās, and ṣalawāt recommended for this blessed period.

Section 1 · Tahlīl with Full Divine Attributes
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdah
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
لَهُ الْمُلْكُ His is the dominion lahul-mulk
وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ and His is the praise wa lahul-ḥamd
يُحْيِي He gives life yuḥyī
وَيُمِيتُ and causes death wa yumīt
وَهُوَ حَيٌّ and He is Ever-Living wa huwa ḥayyun
لَا يَمُوتُ He does not die lā yamūt
بِيَدِهِ الْخَيْرُ in His hand is all good biyadihil-khayr
وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ and He is over all things wa huwa 'alā kulli shay'in
قَدِيرٌ all-powerful Qadīr
There is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner. His is all dominion and all praise. He gives life and causes death — Ever-Living, undying, in Whose hand is all good, over all things Able.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 80, Hadith 6403. Also in Jāmiʿ at-Tirmidhī, Book 45, Hadith 3474.
Section 2 · Shahāda with Qur'ānic Attributes
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ I bear witness: no god but Allah Ashhadu an lā ilāha illallāh
وَحْدَهُ alone waḥdah
لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ no partner for Him lā sharīka lah
إِلَٰهًا وَاحِدًا one God ilāhan wāḥidā
أَحَدًا singular aḥadā
صَمَدًا the Eternal Refuge ṣamadā
لَمْ يَتَّخِذْ He has not taken lam yattakhidh
صَاحِبَةً a spouse ṣāḥibah
وَلَا وَلَدًا nor a child wa lā waladā
I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner — one God, singular, the Eternal Refuge, Who has taken neither spouse nor child.
Qur'ān Combines the Shahāda with attributes drawn from Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ (112:2) and Sūrat al-Jinn (72:3). Formulation found in Bā'alawī awrād compilations.
Section 3 · Shahāda with Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ

وَلَمْ يُولَدْ nor was He begotten wa lam yūlad

I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, alone, with no partner — the One, the Eternal Refuge. He did not beget, nor was He begotten, and there is none equal to Him.
Qur'ān Shahāda prefixed to the full text of Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ (112), Āyāt 1–4.

📖 Reliance, Relief & Remembrance

This file contains phrases of tawakkul (reliance on Allah) and a seven-part du'ā for the relief of debt and hardship. These formulations are drawn from Bā'alawī awrād tradition and are especially recommended during the blessed days of Dhul Ḥijjah as a means of drawing near to Allah through supplication.

Section 5 · Phrases of Sufficiency
حَسْبِيَ اللهُ Allah is sufficient for me Ḥasbiyallāh
وَكَفَىٰ and He suffices wa kafā
سَمِعَ اللهُ Allah has heard samiʿallāh
لِمَنْ دَعَا whoever supplicates liman daʿā
لَيْسَ وَرَاءَهُ beyond Him there is laysa warāʾahu
مُنْتَهَىٰ no limit muntahā
Allah is sufficient for me, and He suffices. Allah hears whoever calls upon Him. Beyond Him there is no end.
Ṣaḥīḥ Attributed to the Bā'alawī awrād tradition. "Ḥasbiyallāhu wa kafā" is also recorded in Ṣaḥīḥ Ibn Ḥibbān and related collections as a phrase of the Prophet ﷺ.
Section 6 · Phrases of Tawakkul
مَنْ تَوَكَّلَ whoever relies man tawakkala
عَلَى اللهِ upon Allah ʿalallāh
كُفِيَ is sufficed kufiya
وَمَنِ اعْتَصَمَ and whoever holds fast wa maniʿtaṣama
بِاللهِ to Allah billāh
نَجَا is saved najā
Whoever places their reliance upon Allah is sufficed. And whoever holds fast to Allah is saved.
Ṣaḥīḥ Bā'alawī awrād compilation. The first half echoes Qur'ān, Sūrat aṭ-Ṭalāq (65:3); the second echoes Sūrat Āl ʿImrān (3:103). Found in Rātib al-Ḥaddād tradition.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part A
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
فَرَجُكَ Your relief farajuka
الْقَرِيبُ the near one al-qarīb
O Allah, Your relief is near.
Mashāyikh Seven-part du'ā for relief of debt and hardship from the Bā'alawī tradition, attributed to compilations of Habīb ʿAbdullāh al-Ḥaddād (RA).
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part B
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
سِتْرُكَ Your veil sitruka
الْحَصِينُ the fortified one al-ḥaṣīn
O Allah, Your protection is an impenetrable fortress.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part C
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
مَعْرُوفُكَ Your kindness maʿrūfuka
الْقَدِيمُ the ancient one al-qadīm
O Allah, Your kindness is ancient and ever-present.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part D
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
عَوَائِدُكَ Your recurring gifts ʿawāʾiduka
الْحَسَنَةُ the beautiful ones al-ḥasanah
O Allah, Your recurring gifts are beautiful.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part E

عَطَاؤُكَ Your bestowal ʿaṭāʾuka
الْحَسَنُ الْجَمِيلُ the beautiful, the lovely al-ḥasanul-jamīl
O Allah, Your bestowal is beautiful and lovely.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part F

O Ancient One in beneficence — Your beneficence has always been.
Section 7 · Du'ā for Relief of Debt · Part G

مَعْرُوفُكَ الدَّائِمُ Your kindness is eternal maʿrūfukad-dāʾim
O Eternal One in kindness — Your kindness is everlasting.

📖 Tahlīl of Infinite Measure & Ṣalawāt

Section 8 is an extended tahlīl in which lā ilāha illallāh is repeated with 21 different measures of creation — nights, days, waves, trees, rain, and more — multiplying the weight of remembrance to an immeasurable degree. Section 9 is a ṣalawāt upon the Prophet ﷺ tied to the signs and moments of Ḥajj, both closing with the formula of infinite multiplication found in Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim.

Section 8 · Extended Tahlīl · Measures 1–4
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
عَدَدَ اللَّيَالِي to the count of nights ʿadadal-layālī
وَالدُّهُورِ and the ages wad-duhūr
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
عَدَدَ الْأَيَّامِ to the count of days ʿadadal-ayyām
وَالشُّهُورِ and the months wash-shuhūr
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
عَدَدَ أَمْوَاجِ to the count of waves of ʿadadal-amwāj
الْبُحُورِ the seas al-buḥūr
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
عَدَدَ أَضْعَافِ to the count of multiples of ʿadadal-aḍʿāf
الْأُجُورِ the rewards al-ujūr
There is no god but Allah — to the count of nights and ages, to the count of days and months, to the count of waves of the seas, to the count of multiples of all rewards.
Mashāyikh Extended tahlīl from the Bā'alawī ḥizb tradition, associated with Habīb ʿAbdullāh al-Ḥaddād (RA) and the Wird al-Laṭīf. The closing multiplier formula echoes Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Book 48, Hadith 2726.

عَدَدَ أَنْفَاسِ to the count of breaths of ʿadadal-anfās
الْبَشَرِ all humankind al-bashar

لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh
عَدَدَ لَمْحِ to the count of glances of ʿadadal-lamḥ
الْعُيُونِ all eyes al-ʿuyūn
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ there is no god but Allah Lā ilāha illallāh

There is no god but Allah — to the count of every human breath, to the count of our sins until they are all forgiven, to the count of every glance of every eye, to the count of all that was and all that shall be, and of His transcendence above all they associate.

There is no god but Allah — better than all they accumulate, in the night as it recedes and the dawn as it breathes, to the count of winds across open lands and rocks, from this day until the Trumpet is blown and until the Day of Judgement — in every moment, forever — to the count of His creation, the pleasure of His Self, the weight of His Throne, and the ink of His words.
Section 9 · Ṣalawāt of Ḥajj upon the Prophet ﷺ
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ O Allah, send blessings Allāhumma ṣalli
عَلَىٰ سَيِّدِنَا مُحَمَّدٍ upon our master Muḥammad ʿalā Sayyidinā Muḥammad

الْعُيُونُ the eyes al-ʿuyūn
بِالنَّظَرِ with sight bin-naẓar
وَتَزَخْرَفَتِ and as the earth is adorned wa tazakhrafat
الْأَرَضُونَ the lands al-araḍūn
بِالْمَطَرِ with rain bil-maṭar
O Allah, send blessings upon our master Muḥammad — as long as eyes continue to gaze, and as long as the earth is adorned by rain.
