Khatm al-Ḥadīth

The Prohibition of Pride

📖 Context & Significance

This narration warns against the severe spiritual disease of pride (Kibr). True pride is defined by the Prophet ﷺ as rejecting divine truth and looking down upon creation, rather than simply appreciating beauty or fine clothing.

Part 1 · The Severe Warning
لَا يَدْخُلُ will not enter Lā yadkhulu
الْجَنَّةَ paradise al-jannah
مَنْ كَانَ he who has man kāna
فِي قَلْبِهِ in his heart fī qalbihī
مِثْقَالُ ذَرَّةٍ an atom's weight mithqālu dharratin
مِنْ كِبْرٍ of pride min kibr
He who has in his heart the weight of an atom of pride shall not enter Paradise.
Ṣaḥīḥ Sahih Muslim, Book 1, Hadith 172 (No. 91a).
Part 2 · Definition of Pride
الْكِبْرُ pride is Al-kibru
بَطَرُ الْحَقِّ rejecting truth baṭarul-ḥaqqi
وَعَمْطُ النَّاسِ and disdain for people wa ʿamṭun-nās
Pride is disdaining the truth out of self-conceit and showing contempt for the people.
Ṣaḥīḥ Sahih Muslim, Book 1, Hadith 172 (No. 91a).
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded in Sahih Muslim, Book 1, Hadith 172. Narrated by ʿAbdullāh bin Masʿūd (RA). Grade: Ṣaḥīḥ. It highlights that appreciating beautiful items does not constitute pride, but arrogance against truth and creation does.
