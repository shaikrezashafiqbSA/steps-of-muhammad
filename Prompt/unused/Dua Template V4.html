Du'ā al-Safar

The Prayer for Travel & Journeys

Safar (سفر) refers to travelling or embarking on a journey. This sunnah supplication is recited when mounting a vehicle or beginning a trip by any means—be it car, plane, or ship—to seek Allah's protection and barakah.

Part 1 · The Opening
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ In the name of Allah Bismi-llāh
In the name of Allah  ·  ×3
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ Praise be to Allah Al-ḥamdu li-llāh
All praise is due to Allah
Part 2 · The Core Supplication
سُبْحَانَ Glory be to Subḥāna
الَّذِي the One who alladhī
سَخَّرَ has subjected sakhkhara
لَنَا to us lanā
هَٰذَا this hādhā
وَمَا and not wa mā
كُنَّا were we kunnā
لَهُ to it lahū
مُقْرِنِينَ capable (of controlling) muqrinīn
Glory be to the One who has placed this (transport) at our service, and we ourselves would not have been capable of that.
Qur'ān From Sūrat az-Zukhruf, Āyāt 13–14.
وَإِنَّا And indeed we wa innā
إِلَىٰ to ilā
رَبِّنَا our Lord Rabbinā
لَمُنقَلِبُونَ are surely returning la-munqalibūn
And surely, to our Lord we are returning.
Part 3 · Praises & Seeking Forgiveness
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ All praise to Allah Al-ḥamdu li-llāh
اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ Allah is the Greatest Allāhu Akbar
All praise is due to Allah  ·  ×3     Allah is the Greatest  ·  ×3
سُبْحَانَكَ Glory be to You Subḥānaka
إِنِّي indeed I innī
ظَلَمْتُ have wronged ẓalamtu
نَفْسِي myself nafsī
فَاغْفِرْ لِي so forgive me fa-ghfir lī
فَإِنَّهُ for indeed fa-innahū
لَا يَغْفِرُ none forgives lā yaghfiru
الذُّنُوبَ sins adh-dhunūba
إِلَّا أَنْتَ except You illā Ant
Glory is to You, indeed I have wronged myself, so forgive me — for none forgives sins except You.
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported in Jami' At-Tirmidhi, Hadith 3446.

This full sequence is recorded in Jami' At-Tirmidhi, Vol. 6, Book of Supplications, Hadith 3446. It was narrated that Ali (RA) recited this upon mounting his animal, laughing afterward because the Prophet ﷺ had done the same to show Allah's pleasure with a servant seeking forgiveness.
