Selawat Nabiyy S.A.W

Blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ · 23 Verses

This selawat opens with the Qur'ānic verse commanding the sending of blessings (33:56), then flows through 23 paired verses — each beginning with Yā Rabbī ṣalli ʿalā Muḥammad and paired with a specific supplication covering intercession, the Companions, family, mercy, forgiveness, hope, light, protection, Paradise, and a blessed end.

Selawat Bushrā

The Glad-Tidings Blessing · صَلَاة الْبُشْرَى

The Selawat Bushrā asks Allah to send complete blessings upon Sayyidinā Muḥammad "the owner of glad tidings" — a blessing that envelops not only the reciter, but their family, children, teachers, students, and the entire community, from this day until the Day of Resurrection.

Selawat Nāriyyah

The Complete Blessing · صَلَاة النَّارِيَّة

The Selawat Nāriyyah (also known as Ṣalawāt al-Tāziyya) is one of the most widely recited ṣalawāt in the world, beloved across North Africa, West Africa, and Southeast Asia. The name "nāriyyah" (fiery) refers to the belief that it burns away worries and opens closed doors. It is commonly recited 41 or 313 times for relief from hardship. The text lists specific benefits: untying knots of difficulty, relief from distress, fulfilment of needs, attainment of desires, a good ending, and rain through the Prophet's blessed face ﷺ.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends one blessing upon me, Allah will send ten blessings upon him." — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 408

Kisah Sang Nabi

The Story of the Prophet ﷺ · Nashīd & Arabic Chorus

This nashīd alternates between an Arabic chorus celebrating the Prophet's radiance and Malay verses narrating key events of his blessed life — from his noble lineage and early losses, through his youth and marriage to Khadījah, to his receiving prophethood at 40. A beloved pedagogical nashīd in Southeast Asian mawlid programmes.

Maḥallul Qiyām — مَحَلُّ الْقِيَام

Standing Salutation upon the Prophet ﷺ

The Maḥallul Qiyām is the standing salutation — the most solemn moment of the mawlid where the congregation rises to their feet. This version contains the Yā Nabī Salām refrain, four verses of praise, the Marhaban welcome section, the Ṭalaʿal Badru salutation, a duʿā of intercession, and a closing with final ṣalawāt.

Qur'ān The Qur'ānic verses (33:56, 48:1–3, 9:128–129) are from the Qur'ān al-Karīm.

Nashīd All selawat and nashīd texts are devotional compositions — expressions of love and longing for the Prophet ﷺ, not ḥadīth narrations.

Ṣaḥīḥ The Selawat Nāriyyah is widely attributed to Muḥammad al-Tāzī. Scholars have affirmed the permissibility of such compositions as expressions of love and tawassul.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends one blessing upon me, Allah will send ten blessings upon him." — Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 408
