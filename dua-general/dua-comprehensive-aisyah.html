Allāhumma innī asʼaluka mina-l-khayri kullihi

Comprehensive Supplication for All Good · Refuge from All Evil · Seeking Paradise & Good in Decree

📖 When This Duʿāʾ Was Taught

The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) personally taught this all-encompassing supplication to ʿĀʾishah (may Allah be pleased with her). It asks for every form of goodness — known and unknown, immediate and delayed — seeks refuge from all evil, requests the specific good asked by the Prophet ﷺ, pleads for Paradise and proximity to it, seeks protection from Hellfire, and ends with a profound request: that every divine decree concerning the believer be turned into pure good.

The Narration Chain & Context

ʿĀʾishah (ra) reported:
"The Messenger of Allah (saas) taught me this supplication..."

ʿĀʾishah bint Abī Bakr (ra)

Full name: ʿĀʾishah bint Abī Bakr al-Ṣiddīq (d. 58 AH / 678 CE). Mother of the Believers, wife of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, daughter of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq. One of the most prolific narrators of ḥadīth, renowned for her scholarship, jurisprudence, and deep understanding of the Qurʾān and Sunnah.

She transmitted over 2,000 narrations and was frequently consulted by the Companions on legal and theological matters. Her narrations are found in all six major ḥadīth collections, with the highest standards of authentication.

Role here: Direct recipient and transmitter of this comprehensive supplication from the Prophet ﷺ herself, establishing a direct, unbroken chain of teaching and practice.

✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by ʿĀʾishah (ra) and recorded by Imām Ibn Mājah in his Sunan (Book of Supplication, Chapter on Comprehensive Supplications). The ḥadīth is transmitted through an authentic chain (Ṣaḥīḥ) as graded by Dārus salām. The supplication is remarkable for its comprehensiveness — it covers all good and all evil, known and unknown, immediate and delayed, follows the Prophet's own requests, asks for Paradise and avoidance of Hell, and culminates in the uniquely profound request that every divine decree be transformed into good for the believer.
