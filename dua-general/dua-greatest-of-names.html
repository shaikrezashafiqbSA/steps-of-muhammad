اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ بِأَنَّكَ أَنْتَ اللَّهُ الأَحَدُ الصَّمَدُ

Allahumma Inni As'aluka bi-Annaka Antallah — The Supplication that Invokes Allah’s Greatest Name

📖 When & Why

The Prophet ﷺ heard a man supplicating using this powerful invocation, which testifies to Allah’s absolute Oneness, Self-Sufficiency, and Eternality. The Prophet ﷺ then declared that the man had asked Allah by His Greatest Name — the Name that, when invoked, guarantees a response and fulfillment of need.

The Narration Chain & Context

‘Abdullāh ibn Buraidah reported from his father:
“The Prophet ﷺ heard a man say: ‘O Allah, I ask You by virtue of Your being Allah, the One, the Self-Sufficient Master, Who begets not nor was begotten, and there is none co‑equal or comparable to Him.’ Then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: ‘He has asked Allah by His Greatest Name, which if He is asked thereby He gives, and if He is called upon thereby He answers.’”

"The Prophet ﷺ heard a man saying: 'O Allah, I ask You by virtue of Your being Allah, the One, the Self-Sufficient Master, Who begets not nor was begotten, and there is none co‑equal or comparable to Him.' Then the Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: 'He has asked Allah by His Greatest Name, which if He is asked thereby He gives, and if He is called upon thereby He answers.'"
‘Abdullāh ibn Buraidah & his father Buraidah ibn al-Husayb

Full name: ‘Abdullāh ibn Buraidah ibn al-Husayb al-Aslamī (d. 115 AH / 733 CE). His father, Buraidah (d. 63 AH / 682 CE), was a famous Companion of the Prophet ﷺ who accepted Islam early and participated in many campaigns.

‘Abdullāh is known as a reliable narrator (thiqah) from the generation of the Successors (tābi‘ūn). He transmitted from his father, ‘Umar, and other Companions. Scholars such as Ibn Ḥibbān and Aḥmad praised his integrity. This narration appears in Sunan Ibn Mājah and is graded Ṣaḥīḥ.

Role here: ‘Abdullāh ibn Buraidah heard this statement directly from his father Buraidah, who witnessed the Prophet ﷺ responding to the man’s du‘ā. The chain is strong and goes back to the Prophet ﷺ without interruption.

✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by ‘Abdullāh ibn Buraidah from his father Buraidah ibn al-Husayb al-Aslamī. Recorded by Ibn Mājah in his Sunan (3857). The chain includes ‘Alī ibn Muḥammad (trustworthy), Wakī‘ ibn al-Jarrāḥ (thiqah ḥāfiẓ), and Mālik ibn Mighwal (thiqah). Graded Ṣaḥīḥ (Authentic) by Dārus Salām’s editorial team. This du‘ā invokes Allah’s Ism al-A‘ẓam (Greatest Name), a means of guaranteed response when used sincerely.
