اللهم أعني على ذكرك وشكرك وحسن عبادتك

Allahumma a'innī 'alā dhikrika, wa shukrika, wa ḥusni 'ibādatika

📖 The Beloved Advice of the Messenger ﷺ

The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) held the hand of Mu‘ādh ibn Jabal (رضي الله عنه), declared his love for him, and gifted him this everlasting supplication. He instructed Mu‘ādh to never abandon this du‘ā after every obligatory prayer — a concise but profound request for divine assistance in three pillars of worship: remembrance, gratitude, and excellence in devotion.

📜 The Narration Chain & Context

Mu‘ādh ibn Jabal reported:
"The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) took hold of my hand and said, 'O Mu‘ādh! By Allah I love you, so I advise you to never forget to recite after every prayer: O Allah, help me remember You, to be grateful to You, and to worship You in an excellent manner.'"

"And from Mu‘ādh (may Allah be pleased with him) that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) took his hand and said: 'O Mu‘ādh, by Allah I love you, so I advise you: never abandon after every prayer to say: O Allah, help me remember You, thank You, and perfect my worship of You.' Narrated by Abu Dawud with an authentic chain."

Ṣaḥīḥ Riyad as-Salihin 1422 | Book 15, Hadith 15 | Abu Dawud (authentic chain)
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded by Imam Abū Dāwūd in his Sunan (no. 1522) with a ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) chain. Imam al-Nawawi included it in Riyad as-Salihin (Book of Remembrance, Chapter on the Excellence of Dhikr, hadith 1422). The chain goes through Mu‘ādh ibn Jabal directly to the Prophet ﷺ, and scholars have unanimously graded its core statement as reliable and actionable.

Mu‘ādh ibn Jabal (مُعَاذ بْن جَبَل)

Full name: Mu‘ādh ibn Jabal ibn ‘Amr ibn Aws al-Khazrajī al-Ansārī. A eminent companion from the tribe of Khazraj in Medina. He embraced Islam at a young age during the second ‘Aqabah pledge. The Prophet ﷺ described him as “the most knowledgeable of my Ummah about what is lawful and unlawful.” He was appointed as governor and judge over Yemen. Died in 18 AH (639 CE) during the plague of ‘Amawās.

Role here: Direct narrator of this hadīth; the Prophet ﷺ held his hand, expressed personal love, and commanded him to preserve and practice this du‘ā after every prayer. His transmission is carried through reliable chains into the Sunan and the Riyad collections.
