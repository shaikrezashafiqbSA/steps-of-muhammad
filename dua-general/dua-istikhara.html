اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْتَخِيرُكَ بِعِلْمِكَ

The Du'ā of Seeking Guidance (Salāt al-Istikhāra) – Taught by the Prophet ﷺ for All Matters

📖 Context of Revelation & Practice

The Prophet ﷺ would teach his companions the istikhāra supplication for all important matters just as he would teach them a Sūrah from the Qur'an. It is performed after two rak'āt of non-obligatory prayer, when a person is considering a decision and wishes to seek Allah's guidance, knowing that He alone knows the unseen outcome.

The Narration Chain & Context

Jābir ibn ʿAbdillāh reported:
"The Prophet (ﷺ) used to teach us the way of doing Istikhara (to ask Allah to guide one to the right action), in all matters, as he taught us the Sūras of the Qur'an. He said: 'If anyone of you thinks of doing any job, he should offer a two rak'at prayer other than the obligatory ones, then say (after the prayer): ...'"

Full name: Jābir ibn ʿAbdillāh ibn ʿAmr ibn Ḥarām al-Anṣārī as-Sulamī (d. 78 AH / 697 CE).

One of the most prolific Companions, narrating over 1,500 ḥadīth. He witnessed the Pledge of al-ʿAqabah, the Battle of Badr, and all major events. Known for his deep knowledge and long lifespan, he was a key transmitter from the Prophet ﷺ.

Role here: Direct Companion narrator of this foundational teaching on istikhāra, transmitting the Prophet's own words and method for seeking guidance.

"O Allah, I seek guidance from You by Your knowledge, and I seek power from You by Your might, and I ask from Your immense bounty. For indeed You have power and I do not, You know and I do not, and You are the Knower of the unseen. O Allah, if You know that this matter is good for me in my religion, my livelihood, and the outcome of my affair (or in the immediate and later aspects of my affair), then ordain it for me, make it easy for me, and bless me in it. And if You know that this matter is harmful for me in my religion, my livelihood, and the outcome of my affair (or in the immediate and later aspects of my affair), then turn it away from me and turn me away from it, and ordain for me the good wherever it may be, and then make me content with it."
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 1166 | Book 19, Hadīth 45 | Vol. 2, Book 21, Hadīth 263 (deprecated)
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded by Imām al-Bukhārī (d. 256 AH) in his Ṣaḥīḥ, Book 19 (Prayer at Night – Tahajjud), Chapter 28: "What is recited in the two Rak'a (Sunna) of the Fajr." The chain is ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) according to the criteria of al-Bukhārī and Muslim. The narration comes via Qutaybah ibn Saʿīd → ʿAbdur-Raḥmān ibn Abī al-Mawālī → Muḥammad ibn al-Munkadir → Jābir ibn ʿAbdillāh al-Anṣārī. This du'ā is universally accepted as a prophetic teaching for seeking guidance in permissible matters.
