Allāhumma innī as’aluka minal‑khayri kullihi

The Comprehensive Supplication · Taught by the Messenger ﷺ to ‘Ā’ishah

📖 Context of this Du‘ā

The Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ personally taught this profound supplication to ‘Ā’ishah (may Allah be pleased with her). It is a comprehensive request for all good — known and unknown, immediate and future — and seeks protection from all evil, while asking for Paradise, refuge from Hell, and the transformation of every divine decree into goodness for the believer.

The Narration Chain & Context

‘Ā’ishah (ra) narrated:
"The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) taught me this supplication..."

"Abū Bakr ibn Abī Shaybah narrated to us, from ‘Affān, from Ḥammād ibn Salamah, from Jabr ibn Ḥabīb, from Umm Kulthūm bint Abī Bakr, from ‘Ā’ishah, that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) taught her this supplication."
‘Ā’ishah bint Abī Bakr (ra)

Full name: ‘Ā’ishah bint Abī Bakr al-Ṣiddīq (d. 58 AH / 678 CE). Mother of the Believers, daughter of the first Caliph Abū Bakr, and one of the most prolific narrators of ḥadīth.

She was renowned for her deep knowledge of the Qur’an, sunnah, medicine, and poetry. The Prophet ﷺ himself taught her this comprehensive du‘ā’ directly. Her narrations are foundational in all major ḥadīth collections.

Role here: Direct recipient of the du‘ā’ from the Prophet ﷺ and primary transmitter in this chain through her student Umm Kulthūm bint Abī Bakr.

Part 1 · Seeking All Good & Refuge from All Evil
اللَّهُمَّ o allah allāhumma
إِنِّي indeed i innī
أَسْأَلُكَ ask you as’aluka
مِنَ الْخَيْرِ of the good mina l-khayri
كُلِّهِ all of it kullihi
عَاجِلِهِ its immediate ‘ājilihi
وَآجِلِهِ and its later wa ājilihi
مَا عَلِمْتُ what i know mā ‘alimtu
مِنْهُ of it minhu
وَمَا لَمْ أَعْلَمْ and what i do not know wa mā lam a‘lam
وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ and i seek refuge in you wa a‘ūdhu bika
مِنَ الشَّرِّ from the evil mina sh-sharri
كُلِّهِ all of it kullihi
عَاجِلِهِ its immediate ‘ājilihi
وَآجِلِهِ and its later wa ājilihi
مَا عَلِمْتُ what i know mā ‘alimtu
مِنْهُ of it minhu

"O Allah, I ask You for all that is good, its immediate and its later, what I know of it and what I do not know. And I seek refuge with You from all evil, its immediate and its later, what I know of it and what I do not know."
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Ibn Mājah 3846 · Book 34, Hadith 20
Part 2 · The Good of the Prophet’s Request & Refuge in His Refuge
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي o allah indeed i allāhumma innī
أَسْأَلُكَ ask you as’aluka
مِنْ خَيْرِ from the good min khayri
مَا سَأَلَكَ what asked you mā sa’alaka
عَبْدُكَ وَنَبِيُّكَ your slave and your prophet ‘abduka wa nabiyyuka
وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ and i seek refuge in you wa a‘ūdhu bika
مِنْ شَرِّ from the evil min sharri
مَا عَاذَ بِهِ what sought refuge in him mā ‘ādha bihi
عَبْدُكَ وَنَبِيُّكَ your slave and your prophet ‘abduka wa nabiyyuka
"O Allah, I ask You for the good that Your slave and Prophet has asked You for, and I seek refuge with You from the evil from which Your slave and Prophet sought refuge."
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Ibn Mājah 3846 · Book 34, Hadith 20
Part 3 · Asking for Paradise & Refuge from Hell, and Transforming Decree
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي o allah indeed i allāhumma innī
أَسْأَلُكَ الْجَنَّةَ ask you paradise as’aluka l-jannah
وَمَا قَرَّبَ إِلَيْهَا and what brings near to it wa mā qarraba ilayhā
مِنْ قَوْلٍ أَوْ عَمَلٍ of speech or deed min qawlin aw ‘amalin
وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ and i seek refuge in you wa a‘ūdhu bika
مِنَ النَّارِ from the fire mina n-nār
وَمَا قَرَّبَ إِلَيْهَا and what brings near to it wa mā qarraba ilayhā
مِنْ قَوْلٍ أَوْ عَمَلٍ of speech or deed min qawlin aw ‘amalin
وَأَسْأَلُكَ and i ask you wa as’aluka
أَنْ تَجْعَلَ that you make an taj‘ala
كُلَّ قَضَاءٍ every decree kulla qaḍā’in
قَضَيْتَهُ لِي you decree for me qaḍaytahū lī
خَيْرًا good khayran
"O Allah, I ask You for Paradise and for that which brings one closer to it, in word and deed, and I seek refuge in You from Hell and from that which brings one closer to it, in word and deed. And I ask You to make every decree that You decree concerning me good."
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Ibn Mājah 3846 · Book 34, Hadith 20
✅ Source & Authenticity

This du‘ā’ is recorded in Sunan Ibn Mājah (3846), Book of Supplication (34), Hadith 20. The narration chain is considered Ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) by Dārus Sallām’s grading. The Prophet ﷺ personally taught this comprehensive supplication to ‘Ā’ishah (ra), encompassing all good, protection from all evil, the best of prophetic requests, Paradise, refuge from Hell, and the transformation of divine decree into goodness.
