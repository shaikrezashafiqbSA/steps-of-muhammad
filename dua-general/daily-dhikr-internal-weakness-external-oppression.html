Daily Shields 🛡: Internal Weakness & External Oppression

Worry, grief, incapacity, laziness, miserliness, cowardice & being overpowered by men

📖 When did the Prophet ﷺ recite this?

This comprehensive du‘ā was among the supplications the Messenger of Allah ﷺ never abandoned. He would recite it regularly — morning and evening, and especially when feeling overwhelmed by life's burdens or fearing the dominance of others. It is a shield against internal weaknesses and external oppression.

The Narration Chain & Context

Anas ibn Mālik reported:
“The Messenger of Allah ﷺ had supplications that he never omitted to recite. He used to say: ‘O Allah, I seek refuge with You from worry, grief, incapacity, laziness, miserliness, cowardice and being overpowered by (other) men.’”

"ʿAlī ibn al-Mundhir informed us from Ibn Fuḍayl, he said: Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq narrated to us from al-Minhāl ibn ʿAmr, from Anas ibn Mālik, who said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ had supplications that he never omitted. He used to say: "
Anas ibn Mālik al-Anṣārī (d. 93 AH / 712 CE)

Full name: Anas ibn Mālik ibn al-Naḍr al-Khazrajī. He served the Prophet ﷺ for ten years as his personal attendant. He was among the most prolific narrators of ḥadīth (over 2,286 narrations). He was the last of the major Companions to die in Basra.

His reliability is unanimously accepted (thiqah thābit). He transmitted from the Prophet ﷺ directly. Scholars like Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal and Yaḥyā ibn Ma‘īn affirmed his authenticity. His narrations appear in all six canonical collections.

Role here: Direct Companion (ṣaḥābī) reporting from the Messenger of Allah ﷺ — the primary source for this du‘ā. The chain through Minhāl and Ibn Isḥāq is graded Ṣaḥīḥ by Dārusalām.

Part 1 · The Complete Prophetic Supplication
اللَّهُمَّ o allah allāhumma
إِنِّي indeed i innī
أَعُوذُ i seek refuge a‘ūdhu
بِكَ with you bika
مِنَ from mina
الْهَمِّ the worry l-hammi
وَالْحَزَنِ and the grief wal-ḥazani
وَالْعَجْزِ and the incapacity wal-‘ajzi
وَالْكَسَلِ and the laziness wal-kasali
وَالْبُخْلِ and the miserliness wal-bukhli
وَالْجُبْنِ and the cowardice wal-jubni
وَغَلَبَةِ and overpowering wa ghalabati
الرِّجَالِ of (other) men r-rijāl
"O Allah, I seek refuge with You from worry and grief, from incapacity and laziness, from miserliness and cowardice, and from being overpowered by (other) men."
Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasa’i 5449 — Book 50, Hadith 22 | Also in Aḥmad, Abū Dāwūd 1541
✅ Source & Authenticity

Recorded by Imām al-Nasā’ī in As-Sunan al-Kubrā (5449) through a chain from ʿAlī ibn al-Mundhir ← Ibn Fuḍayl ← Muḥammad ibn Isḥāq ← al-Minhāl ibn ʿAmr ← Anas ibn Mālik. The ḥadīth is graded Ṣaḥīḥ by Dārusalām and corroborated by narrations in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (6369, 6393), Muslim (2706), and Abū Dāwūd (1541). The text is mutawātir in meaning and practiced by the Prophet ﷺ regularly.
