Du'ā — Lā ilāha illallāh

Post-Prayer Dhikr (After Taslīm)

This supplication was heard from Abdullah bin al-Zubayr on the pulpit. He reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ would recite these words immediately after the Taslīm (concluding the prayer). It is a powerful declaration of Monotheism (Tawḥīd), expressing complete reliance on Allah and sincerity in faith.

Part 1 · Oneness & Sovereignty
لَا no
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
اللَّهُ Allah
وَحْدَهُ Alone
Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdah
There is no god but Allah, alone
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported in Sunan an-Nasa'i. A foundational declaration of the oneness of Allah.
Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339 ↗
لَا no
شَرِيكَ partner
لَهُ for Him
lā sharīka lah
He has no partner
Ṣaḥīḥ Negation of any associates in Allah's divinity, lordship, or names and attributes.
لَهُ to Him
الْمُلْكُ the dominion
وَلَهُ and to Him
الْحَمْدُ the praise
lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamd
His is the dominion, and to Him is all praise
Ṣaḥīḥ Acknowledging Allah's absolute ownership and deserving of all gratitude.
وَهُوَ and He
عَلَى over
كُلِّ every
شَيْءٍ thing
قَدِيرٌ Powerful
wa huwa ʿalā kulli shay'in qadīr
And He is Able to do all things
Ṣaḥīḥ Affirming Allah's omnipotence and control over the entire universe.
Part 2 · Strength & Worship
لَا no
حَوْلَ power
وَلَا and no
قُوَّةَ strength
إِلَّا except
بِاللَّهِ with Allah
lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh
There is no power and no strength except with Allah
Ṣaḥīḥ A declaration of total dependence on Allah for every movement and change in life.
لَا no
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
اللَّهُ Allah
لَا not
نَعْبُدُ we worship
إِلَّا except
إِيَّاهُ Him
lā ilāha illallāhu wa lā naʿbudu illā iyyāh
There is no god but Allah, and we worship none but Him
Ṣaḥīḥ Confirming that worship is a direct consequence of believing in Allah's oneness.
Part 3 · Sincerity & Praise
أَهْلَ Possessor
النِّعْمَةِ of grace
وَالْفَضْلِ and bounty
وَالثَّنَاءِ and praise
الْحَسَنِ the good
ahlan-niʿmati wal-faḍli wath-thanā'il-ḥasan
(He is) the source of blessing, bounty, and all good praise
Ṣaḥīḥ Recognizing Allah as the ultimate source of every good thing we possess.
مُخْلِصِينَ sincere
لَهُ to Him
الدِّينَ in faith
وَلَوْ even if
كَرِهَ detests
الْكَافِرُونَ the disbelievers
mukhlisīna lahud-dīna wa law karihal-kāfirūn
Being sincere in faith to Him, even though the disbelievers detest it
Ṣaḥīḥ A commitment to pure worship regardless of external pressure or disapproval.

This hadith is recorded in Sunan an-Nasa'i and is graded Sahih by Darussalam. It is a Sunnah to recite this after every obligatory prayer to reinforce one's Tawhid and reliance on Allah.

Ṣaḥīḥ Reference: Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339
