Du'ā — Tawḥīd after Taslīm

Post-Prayer Remembrance (Adhkār)

Abdullah bin Az-Zubair reported that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ used to recite these words of Tawḥīd (Divine Oneness) and praise immediately after the taslīm (concluding the prayer). This Sunnah reinforces the believer's absolute reliance on Allah and sincerity in faith after performing the obligatory worship.

Part 1 · Sovereignty & Praise
لَا None worthy of worship
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
اللَّهُ Allah
وَحْدَهُ alone
لَا no
شَرِيكَ partner
لَهُ for Him
Lā ilāha illallāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah
There is none worthy of worship except Allah alone, with no partner or associate.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.
لَهُ to Him belongs
الْمُلْكُ the Dominion
وَلَهُ and to Him
الْحَمْدُ all praise
وَهُوَ and He
عَلَى over
كُلِّ every
شَيْءٍ thing
قَدِيرٌ is Capable
lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamdu wa huwa ʿalā kulli shay'in qadīr
His is the Dominion, to Him be all praise, and He is able to do all things.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.
Part 2 · Strength & Exclusivity
لَا there is no
حَوْلَ power
وَلَا and no
قُوَّةَ strength
إِلَّا except
بِاللَّهِ with Allah
lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh
There is no power and no strength except with Allah.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.
لَا there is no
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
اللَّهُ Allah
وَلَا and we
نَعْبُدُ worship
إِلَّا none but
إِيَّاهُ Him
lā ilāha illallāhu wa lā naʿbudu illā iyyāh
There is none worthy of worship except Allah, and we worship none but Him.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.
Part 3 · Blessings & Sincerity
أَهْلُ The Source
النِّعْمَةِ of blessing
وَالْفَضْلِ and kindness
وَالثَّنَاءِ and praise
الْحَسَنِ good
ahlun-niʿmati wal-faḍli wath-thanā'il-ḥasan
The source of blessing and kindness and the One Who is deserving of all good praise.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.
لَا there is no
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
اللَّهُ Allah
مُخْلِصِينَ sincere
لَهُ to Him
الدِّينَ in faith
وَلَوْ even though
كَرِهَ detest
الْكَافِرُونَ disbelievers
lā ilāha illallāhu mukhliṣīna lahud-dīna wa law karihal-kāfirūn
There is none worthy of worship except Allah, and we are sincere in faith and devotion to Him even though the disbelievers detest it.
Sahih Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339.

Sahih Reported in Sunan an-Nasa'i 1339. Also recorded in Sahih Muslim with slight variations in word order, establishing it as a highly authentic practice for the post-prayer period.
