Du'ā — Allāhumma Anta Rabbī

Supplication of Protection & Tawakkul

Context & Background
📖 Context & Background

This comprehensive supplication combines several of the most powerful themes in Islamic du'ā: declaration of Lordship (rubūbiyyah), absolute reliance on Allah (tawakkul), acknowledgement of His perfect will (mashī'ah), affirmation that all power belongs to Him alone, and finally seeking refuge from the evil of one's own soul and all creatures.

It draws from multiple prophetic narrations and Qur'ānic phrases woven together into a single supplication — a format common in classical compiled adhkār. The phrase Mā shā'allāhu kāna wa mā lam yasha' lam yakun (What Allah wills comes to pass…) is particularly beloved in the tradition as an affirmation of complete surrender to divine decree.

The closing line — inna rabbī 'alā ṣirāṭin mustaqīm — is taken directly from the Qur'ān (Hūd 11:56), the words of the Prophet Hūd (peace be upon him), and serves as a seal of trust: that Allah's path is straight and His governance is just.

✅ Source & Authenticity

This supplication weaves together phrases from multiple authenticated hadith and a direct Qur'ānic verse:

Ṣaḥīḥ The phrase Lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh is reported in both Bukhārī and Muslim, and the Prophet ﷺ described it as a treasure of Paradise. — Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 7386

Ṣaḥīḥ The phrase Allāhumma anta rabbī lā ilāha illā anta, ʿalayka tawakkaltu wa anta rabbul ʿarshil ʿaẓīm is reported by Abū Dāwūd among the morning and evening adhkār. — Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082

Ṣaḥīḥ The phrase Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min sharri nafsī wa min sharri kulli dābbatin anta ākhidhun bināṣiyatihā is reported in the collections of Abū Dāwūd and al-Nasā'ī. — Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5083

The closing verse — "Verily my Lord is on a Straight Path" — is the direct speech of Prophet Hūd (peace be upon him) in the Qur'ān, expressing complete trust that Allah's decree is just and His governance is perfect.
— Qur'ān, Sūrat Hūd (11:56)

📚 The full combined supplication is found in classical morning/evening adhkār compilations, including Ḥiṣn al-Muslim (Fortress of the Muslim) by Sa'īd al-Qaḥṭānī, and is widely taught in traditional Islamic circles as a comprehensive daily protection du'ā. — See full chain at Sunan Abī Dāwūd
