Ruqyah for Severe Pain

Quranic Healing — Part 1 of 2

📖 What is Ruqyah?

Ruqyah (رُقْيَة) is the practice of reciting Quranic verses and authentic prophetic supplications over oneself or another person, seeking healing and relief directly from Allah. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Make use of the two remedies: honey and the Quran." (Ibn Mājah). For conditions such as trigeminal neuralgia — where pain strikes suddenly along the face and jaw — Ruqyah is performed alongside medical treatment as a means of spiritual fortitude and divine reliance. Place a clean hand on the site of pain, recite with firm belief (yaqīn) that all healing (shifāʾ) belongs to Allah alone, and blow gently onto the affected area.

Part 1 · Sūrat al-Fātiḥah — The Opening
📖 As-Shifāʾ — The Cure

Sūrat al-Fātiḥah is known by the title As-Shifāʾ (the Cure). When a companion recited it over a man bitten by a scorpion and he was healed, the Prophet ﷺ confirmed: "How did you know it was a ruqyah?" (Bukhārī 5736). Recite the entire sūrah an odd number of times — ideally 3 or 7 — with your hand placed on the painful area, breathing the intention of healing with each recitation.

Part 2 · Āyat al-Kursī — The Verse of the Throne
📖 The Greatest Verse

The Prophet ﷺ declared Āyat al-Kursī the greatest verse in the Qur'ān (Muslim 810). It affirms Allah's absolute sovereignty over all creation — including the body and its nervous system. Reciting it establishes divine protection and is said to keep a guardian angel over the reciter until morning. For ruqyah, recite once with deep focus, feeling each Name of Allah as a shield around the site of pain.

اللَّهُ Allah Allāhu
لَا إِلَٰهَ there is no god lā ilāha
إِلَّا هُوَ except Him illā huwa
الْحَيُّ the Ever-Living l-ḥayyu
الْقَيُّومُ the Self-Subsisting l-qayyūm
Allah — there is no god except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Subsisting.
Qur'ān Sūrat al-Baqarah (2), Āyah 255.

Neither drowsiness nor sleep overtakes Him.

The Muʿawwidhatān & Prophetic Du'ās — Part 2 of 2

📖 The Blowing Sūrahs

ʿĀʾishah (RA) narrated that whenever the Prophet ﷺ fell ill, he would recite Sūrat al-Ikhlāṣ, Sūrat al-Falaq, and Sūrat an-Nās — known together as the Muʿawwidhāt (the Seekers of Refuge). He would blow into his palms and wipe his body from head to foot (Bukhārī 5748). For ruqyah on nerve pain: recite each sūrah, blow softly into cupped hands, then wipe over the jaw, cheek, or temple where the pain is concentrated. Repeat each sūrah three times.

Part 6 · Du'ā for Pain in the Body
📖 Method of Recitation

Place your hand directly on the site of pain. Begin by saying Bismillāh three times. Then recite the following formula seven times, pausing between each recitation to feel the meaning. This du'ā calls upon the two divine attributes most relevant to pain relief: ʿizzah (might) and qudrah (power). The Prophet ﷺ instructed this exact method to ʿUthmān ibn Abī al-ʿĀṣ (RA) when he complained of bodily pain (Muslim 2202).

أَعُوذُ I seek refuge Aʿūdhu
بِعِزَّةِ اللَّهِ in the might of Allah bi-ʿizzati llāhi
وَقُدْرَتِهِ and His power wa qudratih
مِنْ شَرِّ from the evil of min sharri
مَا أَجِدُ what I feel mā ajidu
وَأُحَاذِرُ and what I fear wa uḥādhiru
I seek refuge in the might of Allah and His power from the evil of what I feel and what I fear.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Book 39, Hadith 2202. Narrated by ʿUthmān ibn Abī al-ʿĀṣ (RA).
Part 7 · Du'ā of Shifāʾ — The Complete Healing
📖 Allāh as Ash-Shāfī

This du'ā addresses Allah by His name Rabb an-Nās (Lord of Mankind) and invokes His attribute Ash-Shāfī (the Healer) — one of the most powerful divine names to call upon in illness. The concluding phrase — a healing that leaves no trace of disease — makes this du'ā particularly suited to chronic and recurring pain conditions. Recite with full presence of heart, as if making direct petition. Recorded in Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (5675) and Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (2191).

اللَّهُمَّ O Allah Allāhumma
رَبَّ النَّاسِ Lord of mankind rabba n-nāsi
أَذْهِبِ الْبَأْسَ remove the harm adhhib il-baʾs
O Allah, Lord of mankind, remove the harm.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 76, Hadith 5675. Narrated by ʿĀʾishah (RA).
اشْفِ heal Ishfi
أَنْتَ الشَّافِي You are the Healer Anta sh-Shāfī
Heal — for You are the Healer.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 76, Hadith 5675. Narrated by ʿĀʾishah (RA).
لَا شِفَاءَ there is no healing Lā shifāʾa
إِلَّا شِفَاؤُكَ except Your healing illā shifāʾuka
There is no healing except Your healing —
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 76, Hadith 5675. Narrated by ʿĀʾishah (RA).
شِفَاءً a healing shifāʾan
لَّا يُغَادِرُ that leaves not lā yughādiru
سَقَمًا any disease behind saqamā
A healing that leaves no disease behind.
Ṣaḥīḥ Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī, Book 76, Hadith 5675. Narrated by ʿĀʾishah (RA).
