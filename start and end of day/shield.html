Bismillāhi Alladhī Lā Yaḍurru Maʿa Ismihi Shayʾ

The Morning & Evening Invocation of Protection • Jamiʿ at-Tirmidhi 3388

📖 Context of this Supplication

The Prophet ﷺ taught this powerful duʿā to be recited every morning and every evening, three times, as a shield against all harm from earth and heavens. ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān (ra) narrated it, and his son Abān later experienced its reality when he missed a single day.

"And Abān had been stricken with a type of semi-paralysis, so a man began to look at him. Abān said to him, 'What are you looking at? Indeed the Hadith is as I reported it to you, but I did not say it one day, so Allah carried out His decree upon me.'"

✅ Source & Authenticity

This ḥadīth is recorded by Imām at-Tirmidhī in his Jāmiʿ (Book 48, Chapter 13, Hadith 3388). He declared it Ḥasan Ṣaḥīḥ Gharīb. The chain goes through ʿAbd ar-Raḥmān ibn Abī az-Zinād from his father from Abān ibn ʿUthmān from his father ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān (ra) directly from the Prophet ﷺ. The duʿā is to be recited three times every morning and every evening. The narration includes Abān's personal testimony of its effectiveness when observed and consequence when omitted.

Abān ibn ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān (d. 105 AH / 723 CE)

Full name: Abān ibn ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān al-Umawī al-Madanī. Born during the caliphate of his father ʿUthmān (ra), he grew up in Medina and became a renowned scholar of ḥadīth, fiqh, and qirāʾāt. He served as the governor of Medina under ʿAbd al-Malik ibn Marwān and later under his son al-Walīd.

He narrated from his father ʿUthmān, Zayd ibn Thābit, and other Companions. His narrations appear in the six major ḥadīth collections. He was known for his piety, reliability, and for preserving this specific duʿā along with its experiential lesson. He died in Medina around 105 AH.

Role here: Direct narrator from his father ʿUthmān (ra), and the living witness to the duʿā's power — he shares how missing it one day led to affliction, proving the ḥadīth's truth through personal trial.
