Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm

Supplication for Morning and Evening — Seeking Allah to Set Right All Affairs

📖 Context & Virtue

This powerful du'ā was taught by the Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ to his daughter Fāṭimah (raḍiya Allāhu ʿanhā) as a comprehensive invocation for entering the morning and evening. It acknowledges Allah’s eternal, self-sustaining nature, seeks His mercy, and asks Him to take charge of every affair—spiritual and worldly—without leaving the servant to their own weakness, even for the blink of an eye.

📜 The Hadith Narration (Chain & Context)

Anas ibn Mālik reports that the Prophet ﷺ said to Fāṭimah:
"What prevents you from listening to what I advise you to say? Say, when you enter the morning and when you enter the evening: Yā Ḥayyu Yā Qayyūm, bi-raḥmatika astaghīth, aṣliḥ lī shaʾnī kullah, wa-lā takilnī ilā nafsī ṭarfata ʿayn."

Anas ibn Mālik (أَنَس بْن مَالِك)

Full name: Anas ibn Mālik ibn al-Naḍr al-Khazrajī al-Anṣārī (c. 612 – 712 CE / 93 AH). He was one of the most prolific companions and narrators of ḥadīth, serving the Prophet ﷺ for ten years as his personal attendant.

His mother Umm Sulaym was among the early Anṣārī women to accept Islam. Anas is known for transmitting over 2,000 narrations, including critical details of the Prophet’s daily life, duʿās, and practices. He resided in Basra later in life and was highly respected among the tābiʿūn.

Role here: Anas ibn Mālik is the direct narrator hearing the Prophet ﷺ address his daughter Fāṭimah. His transmission preserves this morning/evening supplication with its full chain through ʿUthmān ibn Mawhab.

✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by Imām Aḥmad ibn Shuʿayb al-Nasāʾī in Al-Sunan al-Kubrā (vol. 6, p. 176, hadith 10330). The chain: ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Muḥammad ibn Salām ← Zayd ibn al-Ḥubāb ← ʿUthmān ibn Mawhab al-Hāshimī ← Anas ibn Mālik. No explicit grade is provided in this source; some scholars classify the chain as ḥasan due to ʿUthmān ibn Mawhab being trustworthy, while others consider it acceptable for virtuous actions (faḍāʾil al-aʿmāl).
