Sayyid al-Istighfār

The Master of Seeking Forgiveness – Most Superior Du'ā for Repentance

📖 When to Recite

This supreme supplication for forgiveness was taught by the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) as the most excellent way to ask Allah for pardon. It is to be recited in the morning and evening with firm faith, and whoever dies on that day or night will enter Paradise. It acknowledges Allah's lordship, His creation of us, our covenant with Him, and our sins — ending with seeking His exclusive forgiveness.

The Narration Chain & Context

Shaddād bin Aws narrated:
"The Prophet (ﷺ) said: 'The most superior way of asking for forgiveness from Allah is: O Allah, You are my Lord, there is none worthy of worship except You. You have created me, and I am Your servant, and I am faithful to Your covenant and promise as much as I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your blessings upon me, and I admit my sins. So forgive me, for none forgives sins except You.' Then the Prophet added: 'If somebody recites it during the day with firm faith in it, and dies on the same day before the evening, he will be from the people of Paradise; and if somebody recites it at night with firm faith in it, and dies before the morning, he will be from the people of Paradise.'"

Shaddād ibn Aws (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu)

Full name: Shaddād ibn Aws ibn Thābit al-Anṣārī. A distinguished Companion of the Prophet (ﷺ) from the tribe of Khazraj in Medina. He was known for his profound piety, knowledge, and transmission of several key ḥadīth. He passed away in 58 AH (678 CE) in Palestine during the caliphate of Muʿāwiyah.

He was a judge in Jerusalem under Muʿāwiyah and is considered a reliable narrator whose aḥādīth are recorded in the Six Canonical Books, including Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim.

Role here: Direct narrator from the Prophet (ﷺ) of this supreme supplication, quoted by his student Bushayr ibn Kaʿb, forming a ṣaḥīḥ chain through ʿAbdullāh ibn Buraydah and the early traditionists.

✅ Source & Authenticity

This ḥadīth is recorded by Imām al-Bukhārī in his Ṣaḥīḥ (Book 80: Invocations, Hadith 6306). It is graded Ṣaḥīḥ (authentic) by consensus of ḥadīth scholars. The chain is from Shaddād ibn Aws (ra) directly to the Prophet Muḥammad (ﷺ) through reliable narrators including ʿAbdullāh ibn Buraydah and Bushayr ibn Kaʿb al-ʿAdawī.
