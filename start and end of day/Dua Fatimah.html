Yā Ḥayyu yā Qayyūm

Morning & Evening Supplication · Relying on Allah’s Mercy

📖 Context & Virtue

This powerful du'ā calls upon Allah by His names Al-Ḥayy (The Ever-Living) and Al-Qayyūm (The Self-Sustaining). It is recommended to be recited each morning and evening, entrusting all affairs to Allah and acknowledging total dependence upon His mercy, not one’s own strength.

The Narration Chain & Background

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās (d. 68 AH / 687 CE)

Full name: ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās ibn ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib al-Hāshimī. Cousin of the Prophet ﷺ, renowned as “Ḥibr al-Ummah” (The Scholar of the Nation) and “Tarjumān al-Qur’ān” (Interpreter of the Qur’ān).

He was a prolific narrator of ḥadīth and a master of tafsīr, fiqh, and Arabic language. He died in Ṭā’if at age ~71. His narrations are found in all six major ḥadīth collections.

Role here: Direct transmitter of this duʿā from the Prophet ﷺ, preserving it as part of the morning and evening supplications.

O Ever-Living One, O Eternal One, by Your mercy I call on You to set right all my affairs. Do not place me in charge of my soul even for the blinking of an eye (i.e. a moment).
Ṣaḥīḥ Al-Ḥākim 1/545 – Al-Albānī, Ṣaḥīḥ at-Targhīb wat-Tarhīb 1/273
✅ Source & Authenticity

Narrated by ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās (may Allah be pleased with him) and recorded by Al-Ḥākim in Al-Mustadrak (1/545) who declared its chain sound (ṣaḥīḥ). Imām Adh-Dhahabī concurred, and Shaykh Al-Albānī authenticated it in Ṣaḥīḥ at-Targhīb wat-Tarhīb (1/273). It is among the morning and evening supplications compiled in Hisn al-Muslim (Fortress of the Muslim, ḥadīth 88).
