Du'ā — Allāhumma Anta Rabbī

Supplication of Protection & Tawakkul

This supplication weaves together Qur'ānic language and prophetic phrases into a single comprehensive du'ā covering tawḥīd, tawakkul, acceptance of divine will, and seeking refuge — making it one of the most complete morning/evening supplications in the tradition. The closing line is the exact speech of Prophet Hūd (AS) from Sūrat Hūd (11:56), sealing the du'ā with an expression of complete surrender to Allah's just governance.

Part 1 · Lordship & Reliance
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
أَنْتَ You are
رَبِّي my Lord
Allāhumma anta rabbī
O Allah, You are my Lord
Ṣaḥīḥ Opening of the morning/evening du'ā reported by Abū Dāwūd.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
لَا there is no
إِلَهَ god
إِلَّا except
أَنْتَ You
Lā ilāha illā anta
There is no god but You
Ṣaḥīḥ The declaration of tawḥīd (Kalimah) — foundational to all du'ā. Also forms the opening of Sayyid al-Istighfār.
Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 6306 ↗
عَلَيْكَ upon You
تَوَكَّلْتُ I rely
ʿalayka tawakkaltu
Upon You I rely
Ṣaḥīḥ Affirmation of tawakkul (complete reliance), found in the same morning/evening narration.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
وَأَنْتَ and You are
رَبُّ the Lord
الْعَرْشِ of the Throne
الْعَظِيمِ the Mighty
wa anta rabbul ʿarshil ʿaẓīm
And You are the Lord of the Mighty Throne
Ṣaḥīḥ Continuation of the same narration — affirming Allah's supreme sovereignty over all creation.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
Part 2 · Divine Will & Power
مَا what
شَاءَ wills
اللَّهُ Allah
كَانَ comes to pass
Mā shā'allāhu kān
What Allah wills comes to pass
Ṣaḥīḥ Affirmation of divine decree (qadar). This phrase is also found in Sunan Abī Dāwūd among recommended morning phrases.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
وَمَا and what
لَمْ He does not
يَشَأْ will
لَمْ does not
يَكُنْ occur
wa mā lam yasha' lam yakun
And what He does not will does not occur
Ṣaḥīḥ Paired with the preceding phrase — together expressing complete belief in qadar (divine decree).
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
وَلَا and there is no
حَوْلَ power to change
وَلَا and no
قُوَّةَ strength
إِلَّا except
بِاللَّهِ with Allah
الْعَلِيِّ the Most High
الْعَظِيمِ the Most Great
wa lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāhil ʿaliyyil ʿaẓīm
There is no power and no strength except with Allah, the Most High, the Most Great
Ṣaḥīḥ The Prophet ﷺ called this phrase a "treasure from the treasures of Paradise."
Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī 7386 ↗ · Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 2704 ↗
Part 3 · Knowledge & Omnipotence
أَعْلَمُ I know
أَنَّ that
اللَّهَ Allah
عَلَى over
كُلِّ every
شَيْءٍ thing
قَدِيرٌ Capable
aʿlamu annallāha ʿalā kulli shay'in qadīr
I know that Allah has power over all things
Ṣaḥīḥ A Qur'ānic affirmation repeated in numerous verses (e.g. 2:284, 3:29). Also found in the morning du'ā narration.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5082 ↗
Part 4 · Seeking Refuge
اللَّهُمَّ O Allah
إِنِّي verily I
أَعُوذُ seek refuge
بِكَ with You
مِنْ from
شَرِّ the evil
نَفْسِي of my own self
Allāhumma innī aʿūdhu bika min sharri nafsī
O Allah, I seek refuge with You from the evil of my own self
Ṣaḥīḥ Reported by Abū Dāwūd and al-Nasā'ī as part of the evening adhkār.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5083 ↗
وَمِنْ and from
شَرِّ the evil
كُلِّ every
دَابَّةٍ living creature
أَنْتَ You
آخِذٌ hold
بِنَاصِيَتِهَا its forelock
wa min sharri kulli dābbatin anta ākhidhun bināṣiyatihā
And from the evil of every creature whose forelock You hold
Ṣaḥīḥ "Holding the forelock" signifies complete control — an image also found in Qur'ān 11:56. Reported alongside the preceding phrase.
Sunan Abī Dāwūd 5083 ↗
Part 5 · The Closing Seal
إِنَّ verily
رَبِّي my Lord
عَلَى is on
صِرَاطٍ a path
مُسْتَقِيمٍ straight
inna rabbī ʿalā ṣirāṭin mustaqīm
Verily, my Lord is on a Straight Path
Qur'ān Direct words of Prophet Hūd (AS) — an expression of total trust in Allah's just and perfect governance.
Sūrat Hūd (11:56) · Quran.com ↗

This supplication is compiled from authenticated morning/evening adhkār narrations, primarily reported in Sunan Abī Dāwūd (5082–5083) and graded sound by scholars. It is widely found in classical adhkār collections including Ḥiṣn al-Muslim by Sa'īd al-Qaḥṭānī.
